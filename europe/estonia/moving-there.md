# Moving to Estonia

## Immigration Pathways

### EEA/EU Citizens
- **Free movement rights** — no visa required
- **Up to 3 months**: No registration needed
- **Over 3 months**: Register your place of residence with the Population Register (Rahvastikuregister)
- **Right to work**: Automatic, no work permit needed
- **EU citizen certificate**: Obtain from the Police and Border Guard Board

### UK Citizens (Post-Brexit)
- **Schengen Area**: Visa-free for up to 90 days in any 180-day period
- **Long-term residency**: Requires a residence permit:
  - Work permit (job offer from Estonian employer)
  - Business visa (for entrepreneurs)
  - Family reunification
  - Digital Nomad Visa

### Third-Country Nationals
| Visa Type | Requirements | Duration |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| Work Permit | Job offer from Estonian employer | Up to 2 years, renewable |
| Digital Nomad Visa | Remote work, min income ~€4,500/month | Up to 1 year |
| Start-up Visa | Innovative business, approved by committee | Up to 2 years |
| Student Visa | Enrollment at accredited institution | Duration of studies |
| Family Reunification | Family ties to Estonian resident | Tied to sponsor's status |
| e-Residency | Digital entrepreneur (no physical residency) | 5 years (renewable) |

## Digital Nomad / Remote Work
Estonia introduced the **Digital Nomad Visa** in 2022:
- For remote workers employed outside Estonia
- Minimum income: ~€4,500/month (gross)
- Valid for up to 1 year
- Can apply from abroad or while in Estonia on a tourist visa
- Covers family members
- Fast processing (15-30 days)

### e-Residency
Estonia's unique **e-Residency program** allows digital entrepreneurs to:
- Register an EU-based company remotely
- Access Estonian e-services and digital banking
- Use the digital ID card for signing documents
- Does NOT confer physical residency or right to live in Estonia
- Excellent for digital nomads who want an EU business presence

## Moving Checklist

### Before Moving
- [ ] **Passport**: Valid for duration of stay
- [ ] **Visa**: Apply well in advance (1-3 months processing)
- [ ] **Healthcare**: Register with Estonian Health Insurance Fund
- [ ] **HRT**: Get 3-6 month supply and doctor's letter
- [ ] **Banking**: Consider LHV, Swedbank, or SEB (e-residents can open accounts remotely)
- [ ] **Housing**: Research rental market (kv.ee, city24.ee)
- [ ] **Language**: Basic Estonian helpful; English widely spoken in Tallinn
- [ ] **e-Residency**: Apply if relevant (separate from physical residency)

### After Arrival
- [ ] Register residence with Population Register (within 3 months for EU)
- [ ] Apply for Estonian ID card (isikutunnistus) or digital ID
- [ ] Register with the Health Insurance Fund (Haigekassa)
- [ ] Open local bank account
- [ ] Get local SIM card (Telia, Elisa, Tele2)
- [ ] Connect with LGBTQ+ community (Trans in Estonia, LGBTQ+ association)
- [ ] Register for healthcare with a GP (perearst)

## Practical Information

### Cost of Living
- **Low to moderate** compared to Western Europe
- Monthly rent (1-bed): €400-800 (Tallinn), €300-600 (Tartu)
- Monthly groceries: €300-500
- Public transport: €30/month (Tallinn free for residents)
- Dining out: €10-20 per meal

### Best Cities for LGBTQ+ Community
| City | LGBTQ+ Scene | Trans Healthcare |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Tallinn** | Good (growing scene) | Best (PERH) |
| **Tartu** | Moderate | Good (University Hospital) |
| **Pärnu** | Limited | Limited |

### Climate
- **Winters**: Cold (-5°C to -15°C), limited daylight (6 hours)
- **Summers**: Mild (15°C to 25°C), white nights
- **Best time to arrive**: Late spring/summer (May-September)

### Language
- Official language: Estonian (Uralic, not Indo-European — challenging)
- Russian: Widely spoken, especially among older generations
- English: Widely spoken in Tallinn and among younger people
- Free Estonian language courses available for immigrants
