## Legal Overview

Taiwan is the most progressive country for LGBTQ+ rights in Asia. Same-sex marriage was legalised in 2019 (first in Asia). However, trans-specific legal protections lag behind.

- **Gender Recognition:** Medicalised. Surgery is currently required for legal gender marker change on ID documents. Activist groups are actively challenging this. There is no third gender option.
- **Anti-Discrimination Law:** The 2023 legal amendments provide protections based on sexual orientation and gender identity in education and some services. Employment protections are partial.
- **Hate Crime Laws:** Hate crimes based on sexual orientation or gender identity are recognised.
- **Conversion Therapy:** Not banned.


## Healthcare

- **HRT Access:** Informed consent available at some clinics in Taipei. Many endocrinologists still require therapy letters. Private clinics are more flexible.
- **Cost:** TWD 1,000–3,000/month (≈ €30–90).

## Cost of Living (Taipei)

1-bedroom: TWD 15,000–30,000/month (≈ €450–900). Monthly budget: TWD 35,000–60,000 (≈ €1,050–1,800).


## Key Considerations
- **Surgery requirement** for gender recognition is a major barrier
- **Safety** is excellent — Taiwan is very safe for LGBTQ+ people
- **Geopolitical risk** is a consideration for long-term planning
- **Mandarin essential** for daily life and healthcare
- **Asylum:** Not available for LGBTQ+ claims
