# Healthcare & HRT in Ireland

## Access Model

Ireland operates a gatekeeping model for transgender healthcare. The primary public pathway is through the National Gender Service (NGS) in Dublin, which requires a referral from a GP followed by a lengthy assessment process. Wait times for a first appointment at the NGS have historically been 3-5 years, though recent reforms aim to reduce this.

Alternatives include private endocrinologists who may prescribe under informed consent, and the GenderGP service (online, UK-based). Some GPs are willing to prescribe HRT under shared care agreements with private specialists.

## Key Clinics

| Clinic | Location | Services |
|--------|----------|----------|
| National Gender Service (NGS) | Dublin | Public gender clinic, HRT, surgery referrals |
| GenderGP | Online | Remote HRT prescribing, international |
| Private Endos (Various) | Dublin, Cork | Private HRT, shorter wait times |
| TENI Healthcare Directory | Nationwide | Trans-friendly GP listings |

## Prescription Portability

As an EU member, Ireland recognises prescriptions from other EU/EEA countries. Non-EU arrivals need an Irish doctor to convert their prescription. A medical history in English is essential. Many GPs listed with TENI are familiar with transgender healthcare.

A 3-month supply of HRT for personal use is generally permitted with a valid prescription. Note that Ireland has strict medicine import regulations — check with the HPRA before moving larger quantities.

## Surgical Options

Gender-affirming surgeries are available through the NGS with public funding, but wait times are very long (3-5+ years). Many Irish trans people travel abroad for surgery (UK, Belgium, Germany, Thailand). Some surgeries may be covered under the EU Cross-Border Healthcare Directive.
