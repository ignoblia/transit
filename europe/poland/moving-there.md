# Moving to Poland

## Immigration Pathways

### EEA/EU Citizens
- **Free movement rights** — no visa required
- **Up to 3 months**: No registration needed
- **Over 3 months**: Register with the Voivodeship Office (Urząd Wojewódzki)
- **Right to work**: Automatic
- **PESEL number**: Required for all services

### UK Citizens (Post-Brexit)
- **Schengen Area**: Visa-free for up to 90 days in any 180-day period
- **Long-term residency**: Requires a residence permit:
  - Work permit (job offer from Polish employer)
  - Student visa (enrollment at Polish university)
  - Family reunification
  - Poland Business Harbour (tech sector program)

### Third-Country Nationals
| Visa Type | Requirements | Duration |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| Work Permit | Job offer from Polish employer | Up to 3 years, renewable |
| Student Visa | Enrollment at accredited institution | Duration of studies |
| Family Reunification | Family ties to Polish resident | Tied to sponsor's status |
| EU Blue Card | Highly skilled (salary ~€30,000+/year) | Up to 3 years |
| Poland Business Harbour | Tech workers, IT professionals | Up to 2 years |

## Digital Nomad / Remote Work
Poland does **not have a dedicated digital nomad visa**. Options:
- **Work visa** if employed by a Polish company
- **Tourist visa** for up to 90 days (no work permitted)
- **Poland Business Harbour** for tech sector remote workers
- Some remote workers use the **short-term work visa** for specific projects

## Moving Checklist

### Before Moving
- [ ] **Passport**: Valid for duration of stay
- [ ] **Visa**: Apply 2-3 months in advance (non-EU)
- [ ] **Healthcare**: Private health insurance initially; register with NFZ after residency
- [ ] **HRT**: Get 6-month supply and doctor's letter (translated to Polish)
- [ ] **Banking**: Consider PKO BP, ING, mBank, or Revolut
- [ ] **Housing**: Research rental market (otodom.pl, olx.pl)
- [ ] **Language**: Polish helpful; English moderate in cities
- [ ] **Document translation**: Official Polish translations needed

### After Arrival
- [ ] Register with Voivodeship Office (non-EU) or municipality (EU)
- [ ] Obtain PESEL number (personal ID) — essential for all services
- [ ] Register with NFZ (National Health Fund)
- [ ] Open local bank account
- [ ] Get local SIM card (Orange, Play, T-Mobile, Plus)
- [ ] Connect with LGBTQ+ community (KPH, Trans-Fuzja)
- [ ] Register with sexuology clinic for trans healthcare

## Practical Information

### Cost of Living
- **Low to moderate** — varies by city
- Monthly rent (1-bed): €400-900 (Warsaw), €350-700 (Kraków), €300-550 (Wrocław), €250-450 (Łódź)
- Monthly groceries: €200-350
- Public transport: €25-35/month (major cities)
- Dining out: €7-15 per meal

### Best Cities for LGBTQ+ Community
| City | LGBTQ+ Scene | Trans Healthcare |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Warsaw** | Vibrant, active scene | Best options |
| **Kraków** | Active, growing scene | Good options |
| **Wrocław** | Moderate scene | Available |
| **Poznań** | Moderate scene | Available |
| **Gdańsk** | Moderate scene | Limited |

#### Cities to Avoid
Many smaller cities and towns in the "LGBTQ+ free zone" regions (especially southeastern Poland) are actively hostile.

### Climate
- **Winters**: Cold (-5°C to -10°C), snow common, limited daylight
- **Summers**: Mild to warm (15-25°C)
- **Best time to arrive**: Late spring/summer (May-September)

### Language
- Official language: Polish (West Slavic)
- English: Moderate in cities, limited in rural areas
- Polish is challenging to learn but essential for bureaucracy
- Free Polish language courses available for immigrants
