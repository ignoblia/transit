Healthcare & HRT in Iceland ​

Informed Consent Model ​

Iceland operates an informed consent model for hormone replacement therapy (HRT). This means:

  • No psychiatric diagnosis required to access HRT
  • Patients must be 18+ (16-18 with parental consent)
  • Treatment decisions made jointly between patient and endocrinologist
  • Focus on patient autonomy and informed decision-making

Accessing HRT ​

Step 1: Initial Consultation ​

  • Contact Landspítali University Hospital, Endocrinology Department
  • Primary referral can be from a general practitioner (GP) or self-referral
  • English-speaking staff available

Step 2: Assessment ​

  • Medical history review
  • Discussion of treatment goals and potential effects
  • Blood tests for hormone levels
  • Fertility preservation options discussed

Step 3: Treatment Commencement ​

  • Hormones prescribed after informed consent consultation
  • Follow-up appointments every 3-6 months initially
  • Ongoing monitoring of hormone levels and health markers

Prescription Portability ​

  • EU/EEA prescriptions: Valid in Iceland under mutual recognition
  • Non-EU prescriptions: May require verification by Icelandic doctor
  • Carrying medication: Keep in original packaging with prescription labels
  • Import: Carry a doctor's letter explaining the medication and necessity

Surgical Options ​

  • Gender-affirming surgeries are available through the public healthcare system
  • Referral required from Landspítali endocrinology team
  • Wait times can be significant (6-12 months for consultation)
  • Some surgeries available through private providers
  • Many Icelanders travel abroad for surgery (Thailand, Spain, Germany)

Costs ​

  • Public healthcare: HRT and consultations are heavily subsidized
  • Prescription costs: Around 1,000-3,000 ISK per month ($7-22 USD)
  • Private care: More expensive but shorter wait times
