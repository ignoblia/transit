# Moving Checklists for Transgender Individuals

Relocating internationally as a transgender person requires careful planning. These checklists help ensure you don't miss important steps.

## Pre-Move Planning Checklist

### 1. Documentation & Legal
- [ ] Research destination country's gender recognition laws
- [ ] Check if your current gender marker will be recognized
- [ ] Gather essential documents:
  - Passport (valid for at least 6 months beyond planned stay)
  - Birth certificate
  - Current ID/driver's license
  - Medical records (especially HRT prescriptions)
  - Letters from healthcare providers
  - Marriage/divorce certificates (if applicable)
  - Educational/professional credentials
- [ ] Consider getting documents translated and apostilled if required
- [ ] Research name change procedures in destination country
- [ ] Check visa requirements based on your nationality

### 2. Healthcare Planning
- [ ] Research healthcare system in destination country
- [ ] Find trans-friendly healthcare providers/clinics
- [ ] Understand HRT access rules (informed consent vs. gatekeeping)
- [ ] Get prescriptions for current medications (aim for 3-6 month supply)
- [ ] Research prescription portability/transfer processes
- [ ] Consider bringing a letter from your doctor explaining your treatment
- [ ] Check if your health insurance provides international coverage
- [ ] Research cost of healthcare/HRT in destination country

### 3. Financial Planning
- [ ] Research cost of living in destination country
- [ ] Set up international banking options
- [ ] Notify your bank of your move to prevent card blocks
- [ ] Research tax implications of moving
- [ ] Consider maintaining accounts in home country if needed
- [ ] Research money transfer options for regular payments
- [ ] Budget for moving expenses (visas, shipping, flights, etc.)

### 4. Housing & Logistics
- [ ] Research trans-friendly neighborhoods/areas
- [ ] Understand rental requirements and processes
- [ ] Consider temporary housing upon arrival (Airbnb, short-term rentals)
- [ ] Research shipping options for belongings
- [ ] Decide what to ship vs. store vs. sell/donate
- [ ] Research pet relocation requirements if applicable
- [ ] Plan for arrival transportation from airport/train station

### 5. Community & Support
- [ ] Connect with local trans organizations in destination country
- [ ] Join expat groups (both general and LGBTQ+-specific)
- [ ] Research dating apps/social scenes that are trans-inclusive
- [ ] Identify emergency resources (LGBTQ+ hotlines, shelters)
- [ ] Consider language learning if moving to non-English speaking country
- [ ] Plan for maintaining connections with support network back home

## Post-Arrival Checklist (First 30 Days)

### 1. Legal & Administrative
- [ ] Register with local authorities (if required)
- [ ] Apply for residence permit/visa (if not visa-free)
- [ ] Set up local bank account
- [ ] Get local SIM card/phone number
- [ ] Register with local healthcare system
- [ ] Apply for tax ID/number (if required for employment)
- [ ] Learn about local laws affecting transgender people

### 2. Healthcare Setup
- [ ] Schedule appointment with local trans-friendly healthcare provider
- [ ] Transfer prescriptions to local pharmacy (if possible)
- [ ] Establish ongoing care for HRT monitoring
- [ ] Locate nearest LGBTQ+-friendly emergency room/clinic
- [ ] Get information about gender-affirming surgeries availability (if desired)

### 3. Integration
- [ ] Explore your neighborhood and locate essential services
- [ ] Join local LGBTQ+ groups or events
- [ ] Consider language classes if needed
- [ ] Start building local support network
- [ ] Familiarize yourself with public transportation
- [ ] Locate grocery stores, pharmacies, and other essentials

## Special Considerations for HRT During Relocation

### Traveling with Medication
- [ ] Keep medication in original packaging with prescription labels
- [ ] Carry a letter from your doctor explaining the medication and necessity
- [ ] Research destination country's regulations on importing medication
- [ ] Consider getting a longer prescription before moving
- [ ] Plan for storage requirements (some medications need refrigeration)
- [ ] Know generic names of your medications in case you need to refill locally

### Maintaining Treatment Continuity
- [ ] Schedule your last appointment before moving to get refills
- [ ] Research timeline for establishing care in new location
- [ ] Have a plan for potential gaps in care
- [ ] Consider bringing extra supplies (needles, alcohol swabs, etc.)
- [ ] Know what blood tests are required for monitoring and how to get them
