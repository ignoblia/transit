# Moving to France

## Immigration Pathways

### EEA/EU Citizens
Free movement applies. If staying more than 3 months, register at the local préfecture. No residence card is required but proof of employment, study, or resources must be demonstrated.

### UK Citizens
Post-Brexit, UK citizens need a long-stay visa (VLS-TS) for stays over 90 days unless covered by the Withdrawal Agreement (resided in France before 31 December 2020).

### Third-Country Nationals
- **Long-Stay Visa (VLS-TS)**: For employment, study, or family reunification. Valid for 1 year, renewable.
- **Talent Passport**: For highly skilled workers, researchers, artists, and entrepreneurs. Fast-track processing.
- **EU Blue Card**: For highly skilled workers with a salary threshold (~€53,000/year in 2025).
- **Student Visa**: For university studies. Part-time work permitted (964 hours/year).
- **Family Reunification**: For spouses and minor children.

## Digital Nomad / Remote Work

France does not have a dedicated digital nomad visa. Remote workers can apply for the **Talent Passport (Profession Libérale)** for self-employed professionals, or the **VLS-TS Visiteur** if you have sufficient resources (no work allowed). The latter is suitable for retirees or those with passive income.

## Moving Checklist

### Before Moving
- [ ] Obtain necessary visa (non-EU)
- [ ] Arrange health insurance (private until registered with PUMA)
- [ ] Research housing (Paris is expensive; Lyon, Bordeaux, Montpellier more affordable)
- [ ] Gather medical records and HRT documentation (translated to French)
- [ ] Learn basic French (essential for administration)
- [ ] Notify your bank and redirect mail

### After Arrival
- [ ] Validate your visa at the OFII (French Immigration Office) within 3 months
- [ ] Apply for a residence card (carte de séjour) if staying long-term
- [ ] Register with PUMA (health insurance) after 3 months of stable residence
- [ ] Open a French bank account
- [ ] Find a GP (médecin traitant) — declare one for best reimbursement
- [ ] Apply for a French SIM card (Free, Orange, SFR)
- [ ] Connect with OUTrans or Acceptess-T for trans community support

## Practical Information

### Cost of Living
- Monthly rent (1-bed): €800-1,500 (Paris), €500-900 (other cities)
- Monthly budget (single person): €800-1,400 excluding rent
- Public transport: €85/month (Paris Navigo), €30-60 (other cities)

### Climate
Varies by region: Mediterranean south (hot summers, mild winters), oceanic west (mild, rainy), continental east (cold winters, warm summers).

### Language
French is the official language. English proficiency varies — higher in Paris and tourist areas, lower in rural regions. Learning French is strongly recommended for administration, healthcare, and social integration.
