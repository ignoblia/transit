# EU Trans-Friendly Countries

This page provides an overview of transgender rights and protections in European Union member states, highlighting the most trans-friendly countries for relocation.

## Overview

The European Union has made significant strides in transgender rights, though protections vary by member state. This guide focuses on countries with strong legal protections, accessible healthcare, and inclusive societies.

## Top EU Destinations for Transgender Individuals

### 1. Malta
- **Legal Gender Recognition**: Self-determination since 2015 (Gender Identity, Gender Expression and Sex Characteristics Act)
- **Healthcare**: Informed consent model for HRT
- **Protections**: Comprehensive anti-discrimination laws, hate crime laws include gender identity
- **EU Freedom of Movement**: Yes (EU member state)
- **Overall Rating**: 5/5

### 2. Spain
- **Legal Gender Recognition**: Self-determination (Law 3/2023)
- **Healthcare**: Informed consent in many regions
- **Protections**: Strong anti-discrimination and hate crime laws
- **EU Freedom of Movement**: Yes
- **Overall Rating**: 4.5/5

### 3. Portugal
- **Legal Gender Recognition**: Self-determination (since 2018)
- **Healthcare**: Informed consent with some gatekeeping
- **Protections**: Good anti-discrimination laws
- **EU Freedom of Movement**: Yes
- **Overall Rating**: 4/5

### 4. Germany
- **Legal Gender Recognition**: Self-determination (since 2025, Self-Determination Act)
- **Healthcare**: Informed consent (varies by state)
- **Protections**: Strong anti-discrimination laws
- **EU Freedom of Movement**: Yes
- **Overall Rating**: 4/5

### 5. Belgium
- **Legal Gender Recognition**: Self-determination (since 2018)
- **Healthcare**: Informed consent model
- **Protections**: Excellent anti-discrimination laws
- **EU Freedom of Movement**: Yes
- **Overall Rating**: 4/5

### 6. Denmark
- **Legal Gender Recognition**: Self-determination (since 2014)
- **Healthcare**: Informed consent with some requirements
- **Protections**: Strong anti-discrimination laws
- **EU Freedom of Movement**: Yes
- **Overall Rating**: 4/5

### 7. Ireland
- **Legal Gender Recognition**: Self-determination (since 2015)
- **Healthcare**: Improving access, some gatekeeping remains
- **Protections**: Good anti-discrimination laws
- **EU Freedom of Movement**: Yes
- **Overall Rating**: 3.5/5

### 8. France
- **Legal Gender Recognition**: Self-determination (since 2022)
- **Healthcare**: Informed consent with some requirements
- **Protections**: Good anti-discrimination laws
- **EU Freedom of Movement**: Yes
- **Overall Rating**: 3.5/5

## Countries with Developing Protections

These EU countries have basic protections but may have significant barriers:

- **Italy**: No specific gender identity law, relies on court rulings; healthcare access varies by region.
- **Hungary**: Recent legislation has restricted transgender rights (2020 legal gender recognition ban for minors, 2023 ban on legal gender recognition for adults).
- **Poland**: No legal gender recognition procedure; relies on court decisions with varying outcomes.
- **Romania**: Legal gender recognition possible through court, but process is lengthy and costly.
- **Bulgaria**: No specific legal gender recognition procedure; relies on court decisions.

## Important Considerations for EU Relocation

1. **Freedom of Movement**: EU citizens have the right to live and work in any EU member state.
2. **Healthcare Access**: While many countries offer trans healthcare through public systems, waiting times and requirements vary.
3. **Language Barrier**: Consider language requirements for integration and accessing services.
4. **Recognition of Documents**: Ensure your gender recognition documents will be recognized in your destination country.
5. **Asylum Options**: In cases of persecution, some EU countries offer asylum based on gender identity persecution.
