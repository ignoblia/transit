# Legal Rights in Cyprus

## Gender Recognition

Cyprus operates a **medicalized model** for legal gender recognition. The requirements are among the more restrictive in the EU.

### Requirements

- Psychiatric diagnosis of gender dysphoria
- Medical evidence of gender-affirming treatment
- In some cases, **surgery may still be required** (particularly for bottom surgery / sterilization)
- Court application required
- Psychological evaluation
- Minimum age: 18

### Process

1. Psychiatric assessment and diagnosis
2. Medical treatment (HRT and/or surgery)
3. Gather medical reports from specialists
4. Application to the District Court
5. Court hearing with medical testimony
6. Decision typically takes 3-6 months
7. Registration of change with the Civil Registry Department

### Key Issues

- The legal framework is unclear and inconsistently applied
- Some judges may still require evidence of surgery despite European Court of Human Rights rulings
- The lack of clear legislation creates significant uncertainty
- Reform has been discussed but not enacted
- Cyprus was found in violation of Article 8 of the ECHR for its previous sterilization requirement

## Name Changes

- Name changes require a court application
- Medical evidence supporting the name change is typically required
- Processed through the District Court
- Can take 2-4 months to complete
- Legal gender change usually results in name change

## Anti-Discrimination Laws

Cyprus has moderate anti-discrimination protections, largely driven by EU membership:

- **Equal Treatment in Employment Act (2004)** — Transposes EU employment equality directives
- **Gender identity** is not explicitly named in the main anti-discrimination law
- However, court decisions have increasingly recognized gender identity under "sex" discrimination
- **Employment** — Protection in hiring, promotion, dismissal
- **Goods and Services** — Limited protection
- **Housing** — Limited protection

## Hate Crime Laws

- Cyprus has hate crime legislation with enhanced penalties
- Gender identity is recognized as a protected characteristic
- The Law Office and Police have specialized units
- Reporting of transphobic hate crimes is very low
- Public awareness of hate crime protections is limited

## Document Recognition

- Updated gender markers on Cypriot passports and IDs are recognized
- EU standard passports and IDs
- Third gender / "X" marker not available
- Birth certificate update requires the court order
- International recognition of updated documents follows EU standards

## Summary

| Aspect | Status |
|--------|--------|
| Gender Recognition | Medicalized (surgery sometimes required) |
| Surgery Required | Varies by judge |
| Third Gender | Not available |
| Anti-Discrimination | Moderate |
| Hate Crime Protections | Moderate |
