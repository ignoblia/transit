# Moving to Belgium

## Immigration Pathways

### EEA/EU Citizens
Free movement applies. Register at your local commune (gemeente/commune) within 3 months of arrival. You need a valid ID/passport and proof of employment, study, or sufficient resources.

### UK Citizens
Post-Brexit, UK citizens face similar requirements to third-country nationals unless they resided in Belgium before 31 December 2020 (protected under the Withdrawal Agreement). A single permit (work + residence) is required.

### Third-Country Nationals
- **Single Permit (Work Visa)**: Requires a job offer from a Belgian employer. The employer must obtain a labour market test.
- **Highly Skilled Migrant (EU Blue Card)**: For roles requiring higher education with a salary threshold (~€60,000/year gross in 2025).
- **Student Visa**: For enrolment at a Belgian university. Part-time work permitted (20 hours/week).
- **Family Reunification**: If you have a spouse, registered partner, or family member in Belgium.

## Digital Nomad / Remote Work

Belgium does not have a specific digital nomad visa. Remote workers typically use the Single Permit route if employed by a Belgian company, or the freelance/independent status (indépendant/zelfstandige) if self-employed. Proof of sufficient income and health insurance is required.

## Moving Checklist

### Before Moving
- [ ] Obtain necessary visa (non-EU)
- [ ] Arrange health insurance (private or register with mutuelle)
- [ ] Research housing in Brussels, Antwerp, Ghent, or Liège
- [ ] Gather medical records and HRT documentation (translated to Dutch/French)
- [ ] Notify your bank and redirect mail
- [ ] Learn basic Dutch or French (Flemish vs Walloon region)

### After Arrival
- [ ] Register at the local commune (within 8 days for non-EU, 3 months for EU)
- [ ] Apply for residence card (non-EU)
- [ ] Register with a mutuelle (health insurance fund)
- [ ] Find a GP (huisarts/médecin généraliste)
- [ ] Open a Belgian bank account
- [ ] Apply for a Belgian SIM card
- [ ] Register with Transgender Infopunt for local resources

## Practical Information

### Cost of Living
- Monthly rent (1-bed): €700-1,200 (Brussels), €500-800 (other cities)
- Monthly budget (single person): €1,200-1,800 excluding rent
- Public transport: €50-80/month (subscription)

### Climate
Temperate maritime — mild summers (18-23°C), cool winters (0-7°C), frequent rain year-round.

### Language
Belgium has three official languages: Dutch (Flanders, north), French (Wallonia, south), and German (small eastern region). Brussels is bilingual (French/Dutch). English is widely spoken in Brussels and expat communities but less so in rural areas.
