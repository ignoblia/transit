# Healthcare in Norway

## Gender Affirming Healthcare

Norway's gender affirming healthcare is primarily centralised at **Oslo University Hospital (OUS) - Rikshospitalet**, through the **National Treatment Service for Gender Incongruence (NBTS)**. The model has shifted toward informed consent in recent years, though some structured psychological assessment remains.

## HRT Access

Hormone therapy in Norway has become increasingly accessible:
- Self-referral to NBTS or referral through a GP
- Initial assessment (typically 1-3 sessions with psychologist/psychiatrist)
- No longer requires a formal diagnosis of gender dysphoria for hormone access
- Treatment is free through the public system
- Typical wait times: 4-12 months for initial assessment

### Key Clinics

| Clinic | Location | Services | Languages | Wait Times |
|--------|----------|----------|-----------|------------|
| NBTS - Oslo University Hospital (Rikshospitalet) | Oslo | HRT, surgery, psychological assessment | NO, EN | 6-12 months |
| Haukeland University Hospital | Bergen | HRT, referrals | NO, EN | 8-14 months |
| St. Olav's Hospital | Trondheim | HRT, referrals | NO, EN | 8-14 months |
| Private endocrinologists | Oslo, Bergen, Trondheim | HRT (limited, with NBTS coordination) | NO, EN | 1-3 months |

## Surgical Options

Gender affirming surgeries are covered by the public healthcare system:
- **Chest/breast surgery (top surgery)** — Fully covered, wait times 6-12 months
- **Genital surgery (bottom surgery)** — Available at OUS-Rikshospitalet, 1-2 year wait
- **Facial feminisation surgery** — Limited public coverage, typically self-funded
- **Hysterectomy/oophorectomy** — Covered
- **Voice surgery** — Limited availability

The NBTS coordinates all surgical referrals. There is a national waiting list for surgeries.

## Prescription Portability

As an EEA member, Norway recognises prescriptions from EU/EEA countries:
- Valid for up to 30 days
- English or Scandinavian language prescriptions recommended
- Ongoing medications should be re-prescribed by a Norwegian GP
- Emergency supply possible from pharmacy in urgent cases

| Medication | Typical Cost (public) | Pharmacy Availability |
|------------|----------------------|----------------------|
| Oestradiol (oral/gel/patches) | ~€5-15/month (after deductible) | Widely available |
| Testosterone (gel/injectable) | ~€10-25/month (after deductible) | Widely available |
| Cyproterone acetate | ~€8-18/month (after deductible) | Available |
| GnRH agonists | Covered (specialist only) | Specialist pharmacy |

Note: Norway has a high-cost protection scheme (egenandelstak) — once you spend 3,051 NOK (~€260) per year on healthcare, the rest is free.

## Mental Health Support

- **NBTS psychological assessment** — Included in care pathway
- **FRI** — Peer support groups across Norway
- **Harry Benjamin Resource Centre** — Counselling and support
- **Private psychologists** — Gender-competent therapists available in major cities
