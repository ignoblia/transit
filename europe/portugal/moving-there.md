# Moving to Portugal

## Immigration Pathways

### EU/EEA/Swiss Citizens
Portugal grants full free movement rights to EU/EEA/Swiss citizens. Registration with the local town hall (Câmara Municipal) is required for stays over three months.

### Non-EU Citizens

| Visa Type | Description | Requirements | Duration |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|----------|
| D7 Passive Income Visa | For retirees and remote workers with passive income | Passive income ≥ €8,000/year (€760/month), accommodation proof | 1 year, renewable, leads to residency |
| D8 Digital Nomad Visa | For remote workers | Remote work proof, income ≥ €3,280/month | 1 year, renewable |
| D2 Entrepreneurs Visa | For business owners and freelancers | Business plan or freelance proof, minimum income | 1 year, renewable |
| Work Visa (D1) | For employment with Portuguese employer | Job offer from Portuguese company | 1 year, renewable |
| Golden Visa | For real estate investors | Property purchase ≥ €280,000-€500,000 (depending on area) | 2 years, renewable, leads to citizenship |
| Family Reunification | For family of residents | Relationship proof, sufficient income | 1 year, renewable |
| Student Visa | For full-time study | University acceptance, funds proof | 1 year, renewable |

## Key Relocation Steps

1. **Choose your visa pathway** — D7 is most popular for remote workers/retirees
2. **Apply for a visa** — At the Portuguese consulate in your country of residence
3. **Secure accommodation** — Proof required for visa application
4. **Register with the town hall** — After arrival
5. **Apply for a residence permit** — At SEF (Serviço de Estrangeiros e Fronteiras)
6. **Get a NIF (tax number)** — Required for everything: bank account, utilities, phone
7. **Open a bank account** — Required for NIF and residency
8. **Register with SNS (healthcare)** — For public health access
9. **Learn Portuguese** — A2 level required for citizenship

## Cost of Living

Portugal offers a moderate cost of living compared to other Western European countries:
- Rent (1-bedroom): €600-1,200/month (Lisbon); €400-800 (Porto); €300-600 (interior)
- Groceries: €200-350/month
- Public transport: €30-60/month pass
- Health insurance: Private insurance €30-80/month
- Eating out: €10-20 for a meal

## Trans-Specific Relocation Tips

- **Portugal is very safe** — consistently ranks as one of the safest countries globally
- **English is spoken in major cities** and tourist areas, but less so in rural areas
- **Healthcare for HRT is best in Lisbon and Porto**
- **Bring medical records translated into Portuguese**
- **Carry at least 3 months of HRT medication** with a doctor's letter
- **Register with a GP (médico de família)** at your local health centre (centro de saúde)
- **The D7 visa is the most popular path** for remote workers — no job offer needed
- **Consider Porto over Lisbon** — lower cost of living, good trans healthcare, less crowded
- **ILGA Portugal** provides community support and trans-specific resources
- **Lisbon and Porto have active LGBTQ+ scenes**; smaller towns may have less visibility

## Language

- **Official language**: Portuguese
- **English** — Widely spoken in Lisbon, Porto, Algarve, and by younger generations
- **Portuguese** A2 level required for citizenship
- Free Portuguese language courses available through the government (PLA programme)

## Citizenship Timeline

After 5 years of legal residence, you can apply for Portuguese citizenship. Portugal has one of the most favourable citizenship-by-naturalisation policies in Europe.
