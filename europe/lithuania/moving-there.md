# Moving to Lithuania

## Immigration Pathways

### EEA/EU Citizens
- **Free movement rights** — no visa required
- **Up to 3 months**: No registration needed
- **Over 3 months**: Register with the Migration Department (Migracijos departamentas)
- **Right to work**: Automatic
- **Residence registration**: Required at the municipality

### UK Citizens (Post-Brexit)
- **Schengen Area**: Visa-free for up to 90 days in any 180-day period
- **Long-term residency**: Requires a residence permit:
  - Work permit (job offer from Lithuanian employer)
  - Student visa (enrollment at Lithuanian university)
  - Family reunification
  - Start-up visa

### Third-Country Nationals
| Visa Type | Requirements | Duration |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| Work Permit | Job offer from Lithuanian employer | Up to 2 years, renewable |
| Student Visa | Enrollment at accredited institution | Duration of studies |
| Family Reunification | Family ties to Lithuanian resident | Tied to sponsor's status |
| Start-up Visa | Innovative business, approved | Up to 2 years |
| EU Blue Card | Highly skilled (salary ~€30,000+/year) | Up to 4 years |

## Digital Nomad / Remote Work
Lithuania does **not have a dedicated digital nomad visa**. Options for remote workers:
- **Start-up visa** for entrepreneurs with an innovative business idea
- **Work visa** if employed by a Lithuanian company
- **Tourist visa** for up to 90 days (no work permitted)
- Some remote workers use the **short-term work visa** for specific projects

## Moving Checklist

### Before Moving
- [ ] **Passport**: Valid for duration of stay
- [ ] **Visa**: Apply 2-3 months in advance
- [ ] **Healthcare**: Private health insurance initially; register with VLK after residency
- [ ] **HRT**: Get 6-month supply and doctor's letter (translated to Lithuanian)
- [ ] **Banking**: Consider Swedbank, SEB, or Revolut
- [ ] **Housing**: Research rental market (aruodas.lt, domoplius.lt)
- [ ] **Language**: Lithuanian essential for daily life; Russian helpful
- [ ] **Document translation**: Official Lithuanian translations needed

### After Arrival
- [ ] Register with Migration Department (non-EU) or municipality (EU)
- [ ] Obtain personal code (Asmens kodas) — essential for all services
- [ ] Register with VLK (National Health Insurance Fund)
- [ ] Open local bank account
- [ ] Get local SIM card (Telia, Bitė, Tele2)
- [ ] Connect with LGBTQ+ community (LGL, Trans Autonomija)
- [ ] Register with Santaros Clinics for trans healthcare

## Practical Information

### Cost of Living
- **Low to moderate** — affordable EU destination
- Monthly rent (1-bed): €350-650 (Vilnius), €250-450 (Kaunas), €200-400 (Klaipėda)
- Monthly groceries: €250-350
- Public transport: €30/month (Vilnius)
- Dining out: €8-15 per meal

### Best Cities for LGBTQ+ Community
| City | LGBTQ+ Scene | Trans Healthcare |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Vilnius** | Moderate (growing scene) | Only option (Santaros) |
| **Kaunas** | Limited | None |
| **Klaipėda** | Very limited | None |

### Climate
- **Winters**: Cold (-5°C to -15°C), snow common, limited daylight (6-7 hours)
- **Summers**: Mild (15-25°C), long daylight until 10pm
- **Best time to arrive**: Late spring/summer (May-September)

### Language
- Official language: Lithuanian (very distinct, challenging to learn)
- Russian: Widely spoken, especially among older generations
- English: Moderate, more common in Vilnius and among youth
- Free Lithuanian language courses available for immigrants
- Russian is very useful for daily interactions
