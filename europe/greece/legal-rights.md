# Legal Rights in Greece

## Gender Recognition
The **Law 4491/2017** reformed gender recognition in Greece:

- **Requirements**: Diagnosis of gender dysphoria from a licensed psychiatrist or endocrinologist
- **Surgery**: Explicitly **not required** — this was a landmark change from pre-2017 law
- **Age**: 18+ can apply; 15-17 with parental consent and court approval
- **Process**: Application to the Civil Registry (Ληξιαρχείο) with medical certificate
- **Timeframe**: Typically 3-6 months

### Key Reform Details
- Law 4491/2017 removed the requirement for sex reassignment surgery
- Also removed the requirement for sterilization (previously mandatory)
- Still requires a medical diagnosis (gatekept by medical professionals)
- Non-binary recognition is not explicitly provided for

## Name Changes
- Name change is processed alongside gender recognition at the Civil Registry
- No separate medical requirements beyond those for gender recognition
- Court decision required for minors
- Typically processed within the same timeframe as gender marker change

## Anti-Discrimination Laws
Greece has trans-inclusive anti-discrimination protections covering:

- **Employment**: Law 4443/2016 prohibits discrimination based on gender identity
- **Education**: Equal access protections
- **Healthcare**: Cannot be denied treatment on the basis of gender identity
- **Goods and Services**: Protected under EU-derived legislation

Enforcement is improving but remains inconsistent, especially outside Athens.

## Hate Crime Laws
- Gender identity is covered under hate crime legislation in the Greek Penal Code
- The **Racist Violence Recording Network** monitors and reports incidents
- Police have specialized departments for handling hate crimes in Athens and Thessaloniki
- Prosecution rates are low but awareness is growing

## Document Recognition
- Updated passports and ID cards are recognized internationally
- Greek identity cards do not show gender explicitly (removed in 2020)
- Passports show updated gender marker
- Previous documents are sealed and not publicly accessible
