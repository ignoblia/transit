# Moving to Finland

## Immigration Pathways

### EEA/EU Citizens
Free movement applies. Register your right of residence at the Digital and Population Data Services Agency (DVV) within 3 months. Proof of employment, study, or sufficient resources is required.

### UK Citizens
Post-Brexit, UK citizens need a residence permit unless covered by the Withdrawal Agreement (resided in Finland before 31 December 2020). The same rules as third-country nationals apply.

### Third-Country Nationals
- **Work Visa (Residence Permit for Employment)**: Requires a valid job offer. Labour market testing may apply.
- **EU Blue Card**: For highly skilled workers with a salary threshold (~€57,000/year in 2025).
- **Startup Permit**: For entrepreneurs with a business idea evaluated by Business Finland. Two-year permit, renewable.
- **ICT Specialists**: For intra-company transfers.
- **Family Ties**: For spouses, registered partners, and cohabiting partners (minimum 2 years relationship).
- **Student Visa**: For university studies. Part-time work permitted (up to 30 hours/week).

## Digital Nomad / Remote Work

Finland does not have a specific digital nomad visa. The Startup permit is the closest option for remote entrepreneurs. Self-employed individuals can apply for a residence permit for self-employment if they can demonstrate sufficient income and business viability.

## Moving Checklist

### Before Moving
- [ ] Obtain necessary residence permit (non-EU)
- [ ] Apply for a Finnish personal identity code appointment
- [ ] Arrange health insurance (required for residence permit application)
- [ ] Research housing in Helsinki, Tampere, Turku, or Oulu
- [ ] Gather medical records and HRT documentation (translated to Finnish, Swedish, or English)
- [ ] Notify your bank and redirect mail

### After Arrival
- [ ] Register with the DVV (Digital and Population Data Services Agency)
- [ ] Receive personal identity code (henkilötunnus)
- [ ] Register with Kela (social insurance) for healthcare coverage
- [ ] Open a Finnish bank account
- [ ] Find a GP (terveyskeskus)
- [ ] Apply for a Finnish SIM card
- [ ] Connect with Trasek or Seta for community resources

## Practical Information

### Cost of Living
- Monthly rent (1-bed): €800-1,300 (Helsinki), €600-900 (Tampere, Turku)
- Monthly budget (single person): €900-1,500 excluding rent
- Public transport: €60-80/month (HSL Helsinki region)

### Climate
Continental with cold winters (-5 to -20°C) and mild summers (15-25°C). Northern Finland has extreme seasonal variation with midnight sun and polar night.

### Language
Finnish and Swedish are official languages. Finnish is notoriously difficult. English is very widely spoken — most Finns are fluent, especially in cities and among younger people. Government services are available in English.
