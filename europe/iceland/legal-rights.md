# Legal Rights in Iceland

## Gender Recognition
The **2019 Gender Autonomy Act** (Sjálfræðislög) allows self-determination of gender without any medical intervention.

- **Age**: 18+ can self-declare; 16-18 with parental consent
- **Medical Requirements**: None — no diagnosis, surgery, or hormone therapy required
- **Process**: Simple administrative declaration through Registers Iceland
- **Third Gender**: Legal recognition of non-binary genders also available

## Name Changes
- Simple administrative process through **Registers Iceland** (Þjóðskrá Íslands)
- No court order or medical approval needed
- Can be done online or in person
- Usually processed within 1-2 weeks

## Anti-Discrimination Laws
Comprehensive protections based on gender identity in:
- **Employment** (hiring, promotion, workplace environment)
- **Housing** (rental, purchase, accommodation)
- **Education** (schools, universities)
- **Public Accommodations** (restaurants, hotels, services)
- **Healthcare** (access to medical services)

## Hate Crime Laws
Gender identity is **explicitly included** as a protected characteristic in hate crime legislation, carrying enhanced penalties.

## Document Recognition
- Updated gender markers on passports and IDs are recognized internationally
- Iceland follows international standards for document recognition
- No requirement to disclose previous gender marker
