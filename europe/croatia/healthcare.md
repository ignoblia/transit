# Healthcare in Croatia

## Gender Affirming Healthcare

Croatia operates a **gatekeeping model** for trans healthcare with very limited services. Most trans-specific care is centralised in **Zagreb**, with few providers outside the capital.

## HRT Access

### Requirements

- Psychiatric diagnosis of gender dysphoria
- Referral to an endocrinologist
- Minimum assessment period before HRT
- Regular follow-up appointments
- Ongoing psychiatric involvement

### Process

1. **Psychiatric Assessment** — At the University Hospital Centre Zagreb (KBC Zagreb) or a private psychiatrist with trans experience
2. **Diagnosis** — Gender dysphoria diagnosis (ICD-11)
3. **Referral** — To an endocrinologist for HRT
4. **HRT Initiation** — Hormones prescribed after medical evaluation
5. **Monitoring** — Regular blood tests and check-ups every 3-6 months

### Key Clinics

| Clinic | Location | Services | Wait Times |
|--------|----------|----------|------------|
| KBC Zagreb - Psychiatric Clinic | Zagreb | Diagnosis, assessment | 3-6 months |
| KBC Zagreb - Endocrinology | Zagreb | HRT, monitoring | 3-6 months |
| KBC Rijeka | Rijeka | Limited trans services | 4-8 months |
| KBC Split | Split | Very limited trans services | 6-12 months |
| KBC Osijek | Osijek | Minimal trans services | N/A |

### Challenges

- Very few specialists with trans healthcare experience
- Long waiting times for appointments
- Limited availability outside Zagreb
- Many healthcare professionals lack training in trans health
- Language barriers (limited English in some settings)
- Stigma and discrimination concerns in rural healthcare settings

## Prescription Portability

- **EU member** — EU prescription cross-border rules apply
- EU/EEA prescriptions valid for up to 30 days
- Croatian Health Insurance Fund (HZZO) covers prescriptions
- Non-EU prescriptions require verification
- HRT medications are generally available at pharmacies

## Insurance Coverage

Croatia has **mandatory health insurance (HZZO)** :

- Basic coverage includes trans-related care
- HRT medications partially covered (prescription fee)
- Surgery coverage requires medical necessity approval
- Private insurance is supplemental
- Out-of-pocket costs are relatively low

## Surgical Options

- Gender-affirming surgery is available in Croatia but very limited
- Most trans people travel abroad for surgery (Serbia, Thailand, Spain, Germany)
- Insurance may cover partial costs of overseas surgery with prior approval
- Top surgery: available in Zagreb, wait 1-2 years
- Bottom surgery: very limited availability in Croatia
- FFS and other surgeries: not available locally

## Mental Health Support

- **KBC Zagreb** — Psychiatric support included in assessment
- **TransAid Croatia** — Peer support and advocacy
- **Zagreb Pride** — Community events and support
- **LORI Rijeka** — LGBTQ+ counselling
- Private therapists: very few with trans experience

## Costs

| Service | Typical Cost | Insurance Coverage |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| GP visit | Covered | Full |
| Psychiatric assessment | Covered | Full |
| Endocrinology consult | Covered | Full |
| HRT (monthly) | €10-30 | Partial (prescription fee) |
| Blood tests | Covered | Full |
| Private therapy | €30-60 | Not covered |
