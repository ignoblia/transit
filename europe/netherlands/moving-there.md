# Moving to the Netherlands

## Immigration Pathways

### EU/EEA/Swiss Citizens
The Netherlands grants free movement to EU/EEA/Swiss citizens. Registration is required for stays over four months.

### Non-EU Citizens

| Visa Type | Description | Requirements | Duration |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|----------|
| Highly Skilled Migrant (Kennismigrant) | For skilled workers with job offer | Job offer, salary threshold (€5,008/month 30+ / €3,672/month under 30) | Up to 5 years |
| EU Blue Card | For highly qualified professionals | Degree + salary threshold (approx. €65,000/year) | Up to 4 years |
| DAFT (Dutch American Friendship Treaty) | For US citizens starting a business | €4,500 investment in Dutch business | 2 years, renewable |
| Orientation Year (Zoekjaar) | For recent graduates from top 200 universities | Degree from qualifying institution | 1 year |
| Work Visa (GVVA) | For employment | Combined work/residence permit | 1 year, renewable |
| Family Reunification | For family of residents | Relationship proof, sufficient income | 1 year, renewable |

## Key Relocation Steps

1. **Find housing** — Housing crisis is severe; start searching months in advance
2. **Register with the municipality (BRP)** — Required within 5 days of arrival (for long stays)
3. **Apply for a residence permit** — Non-EU citizens apply through the IND before arrival
4. **Register for health insurance** — Mandatory within 4 months (€100-150/month)
5. **Open a bank account** — BSN (citizen service number) required first
6. **Get a BSN** — Registered at the municipality
7. **Learn Dutch** — Essential for integration and citizenship (A2 level for permanent residence, B1 for citizenship)

## Cost of Living

High, especially in major cities:
- Rent (1-bedroom): €1,200-2,000/month (Amsterdam); €900-1,400 (other cities)
- Groceries: €350-550/month
- Public transport: €100-150/month pass
- Health insurance: €100-150/month (mandatory)
- Healthcare deductible (eigen risico): €385/year

## Trans-Specific Relocation Tips

- **Trans-friendly healthcare** is concentrated in UMC cities (Amsterdam, Groningen)
- **English is widely spoken** — nearly all Dutch people speak fluent English
- **Bring medical records translated into Dutch or English**
- **Carry at least 3 months of HRT medication** with a doctor's letter
- **Register with a GP (huisarts)** on arrival; they are the gateway to specialist care
- **Join Transgender Network Nederland** for community and advocacy
- **Amsterdam is the most trans-friendly city** but also the most expensive
- **Consider Groningen** for shorter healthcare wait times and lower rent

## Language

- **Official language**: Dutch
- **English** — Widely spoken, over 90% fluency
- **Dutch** required for permanent residence (A2) and citizenship (B1)
- Free language courses are available through municipalities
