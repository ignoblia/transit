# Legal Rights in Hungary

## Gender Recognition
Hungary **completely banned legal gender recognition** in 2023 after previously banning it for minors in 2020:

- **Current status**: No legal gender change is possible under any circumstances
- **2020 ban**: Government decree prohibited gender marker changes on official documents for anyone born with "constitutional gender identity"
- **2023 complete ban**: Any remaining pathways were closed — no court or administrative route exists
- **Impact**: Trans people are forced to carry documents showing a gender that does not match their identity
- **ECHR rulings**: Multiple cases pending at the European Court of Human Rights

### Background
- Before 2020: Hungary had a medicalized but functioning system requiring diagnosis and court approval
- 2020: Government banned gender recognition for minors via decree
- 2023: Complete ban enacted, eliminating all pathways for adults as well
- The ban is part of broader government anti-LGBTQ+ policies

## Name Changes
- Name changes are **still legally possible** in some circumstances
- Processed through the **Civil Registry Office** (Anyakönyvi Hivatal)
- Must demonstrate a "legitimate interest" for the change
- Gender transition is not currently accepted as a valid reason by many registrars
- Outcomes are inconsistent and may require legal appeals

## Anti-Discrimination Laws
- **Employment**: The Equal Treatment Authority (Egyenlő Bánásmód Hatóság) theoretically covers discrimination
- **Gender identity** was previously protected under broad interpretations of the Equal Treatment Act
- **2021 "Child Protection" Law**: Banned "portrayal or promotion of homosexuality or gender reassignment" to minors
  - Affects education, media, and public discourse
  - Created a hostile environment for LGBTQ+ people
  - EU has initiated infringement proceedings against Hungary
- Enforcement of anti-discrimination protections has weakened significantly

## Hate Crime Laws
- The **Criminal Code** includes hate crime provisions for sexual orientation
- Gender identity is **not explicitly included**
- No enhanced penalties for anti-trans hate crimes
- Police and prosecutors rarely apply existing protections for LGBTQ+ victims
- Government rhetoric has been linked to increased hate incidents

## Document Recognition
- Hungarian ID cards, passports, and birth certificates **cannot** be updated to reflect correct gender
- Documents show sex assigned at birth
- Trans people face frequent harassment and discrimination when presenting documents
- International travel can be stressful due to document mismatch
- Some trans people seek gender recognition in other EU countries through legal migration
