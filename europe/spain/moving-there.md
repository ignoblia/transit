# Moving to Spain

## Immigration Pathways

### EEA/EU Citizens
- **Free movement rights** — no visa required
- **Up to 3 months**: No formalities
- **Over 3 months**: Register with Padrón (municipal register) and get NIE (foreigner ID number)

### UK Citizens (Post-Brexit)
- **Schengen Area**: Visa-free for up to 90 days in any 180-day period
- **Longer stays**: Apply for residence permit under:
  - **Gestoría**: Professional support for visa applications
  - **Work visa**: Requires job offer (can be sponsored)
  - **Digital Nomad Visa**: For remote workers (new since 2023)
  - **Non-Lucrative Visa**: Requires proof of sufficient funds (no work allowed)
  - **Golden Visa**: Real estate investment of €500,000+

### Third-Country Nationals
| Visa Type | Requirements | Duration |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| Work Visa | Job offer from Spanish employer | 1-2 years, renewable |
| Digital Nomad Visa | Remote work, min income ~€2,000/month | 1 year, renewable |
| Non-Lucrative Visa | Proof of funds (€28,800+/year) | 1 year, renewable |
| Student Visa | Enrollment in accredited program | Duration of studies |
| Family Reunification | Family ties to Spanish resident | Tied to sponsor's status |
| Golden Visa | €500,000+ real estate investment | 2 years, renewable |

## Digital Nomad / Remote Work
Spain introduced the **Digital Nomad Visa** in 2023:
- For remote workers employed outside Spain
- Minimum income: ~€2,000/month (can be increased)
- Valid for 1 year, renewable for up to 5 years
- Fast-track to residency
- Covers family members

## Moving Checklist

### Before Moving
- [ ] **Passport**: Valid for duration of stay
- [ ] **Visa**: Apply well in advance (2-4 months processing)
- [ ] **Healthcare**: Private health insurance required for some visas
- [ ] **HRT**: Get 3-6 month supply and doctor's letter (translated)
- [ ] **Spanish translations**: Get official translations of key documents
- [ ] **Banking**: Consider Spanish bank account options
- [ ] **Housing**: Research areas (idealista.com, fotocasa.es)
- [ ] **Language**: Spanish essential for integration; English in tourist areas

### After Arrival
- [ ] Register with Padrón (town hall census)
- [ ] Apply for NIE / TIE (foreigner ID)
- [ ] Register with Seguridad Social (healthcare)
- [ ] Assign to local health center (Centro de Salud)
- [ ] Open bank account
- [ ] Get local SIM card
- [ ] Connect with LGBTQ+ organizations (FELGTB+, local groups)

## Practical Information

### Cost of Living
- **Moderate** compared to other Western Europe
- Monthly rent varies significantly by city:
  - Madrid: €800-1,400
  - Barcelona: €800-1,500
  - Valencia: €600-900
  - Seville: €500-800

### Best Cities for LGBTQ+ Community
| City | LGBTQ+ Scene | Trans Healthcare |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Barcelona** | Excellent | Excellent (Hospital Clínic) |
| **Madrid** | Excellent (Chueca district) | Excellent (La Paz Hospital) |
| **Valencia** | Very Good | Very Good |
| **Seville** | Good | Excellent (Virgen del Rocío) |
| **Bilbao** | Good | Good |
| **Granada** | Moderate | Moderate |

### Climate
- **Mediterranean coast**: Hot summers, mild winters
- **Interior**: Hot summers, cold winters
- **North**: Milder, rainier
- **Canary Islands**: Spring-like year-round
