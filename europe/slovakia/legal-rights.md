# Legal Rights in Slovakia

## Gender Recognition
Slovakia has one of the **most restrictive gender recognition systems** in the EU, requiring sterilization:

- **Requirements**: Psychiatric diagnosis + HRT + sterilization (sex reassignment surgery)
- **Process**: File a court application with medical certification
- **Sterilization**: Effectively mandatory — the law refers to "sex reassignment surgery" which is interpreted as requiring sterilization
- **Age**: 18+ only
- **Marriage**: Married individuals face additional hurdles

### The Process
1. Diagnosis from a psychiatrist or sexuologist (Bratislava only)
2. Minimum 1-2 years of documented HRT and real-life experience
3. Sex reassignment surgery (sterilization)
4. File a court application for gender marker change
5. Court decision and civil registry update

**Note**: The sterilization requirement has been condemned by human rights organizations including the European Parliament and the UN. The European Court of Human Rights has found Slovakia in violation of Article 8 (right to private life). Despite this, the law has not been changed.

## Name Changes
- Processed through the **Civil Registry** (Matrika)
- Can be done separately from gender marker change
- Requires a valid reason
- Processed within 30-60 days
- Name change without gender marker change is possible

## Anti-Discrimination Laws
- **Employment**: Prohibited under the Labour Code
- **Goods and Services**: Protected under the Anti-Discrimination Act
- **Education**: General protections apply
- **Gender identity**: Not explicitly listed in anti-discrimination law but covered by interpretation in some cases

The **Anti-Discrimination Act** (Antidiskriminačný zákon) prohibits discrimination based on sexual orientation, but gender identity is not explicitly mentioned. The Slovak National Centre for Human Rights handles complaints.

## Hate Crime Laws
- The **Criminal Code** includes hate crime provisions
- Sexual orientation is a protected characteristic
- Gender identity is **not explicitly mentioned**
- Enforcement is limited — police training on LGBTQ+ issues is minimal
- Far-right groups are active in some regions

## Document Recognition
- Updated passports and ID cards recognized internationally
- Previous documents are archived
- The sterilization requirement means only those who undergo surgery can access updated documents
- People who cannot or will not undergo sterilization cannot change their legal gender
