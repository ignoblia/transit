# Moving to Greece

## Immigration Pathways

### EEA/EU Citizens
- **Free movement rights** — no visa required
- **Up to 3 months**: No formalities
- **Over 3 months**: Register with the municipality (Δήμος) and obtain a Registration Certificate (Πιστοποιητικό Εγγραφής)
- **Right to work**: Automatic, no work permit needed
- **AFM (Tax Number)**: Required for employment and many services

### UK Citizens (Post-Brexit)
- **Schengen Area**: Visa-free for up to 90 days in any 180-day period
- **Longer stays**: Apply for residence permit under:
  - **Work visa**: Job offer from Greek employer
  - **Digital Nomad Visa**: Available since 2021
  - **Financial Independence Visa**: Proof of sufficient income (pensioners, investors)
  - **Golden Visa**: Real estate investment of €250,000+
  - **Family Reunification**: Family ties to Greek resident

### Third-Country Nationals
| Visa Type | Requirements | Duration |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| Work Visa | Job offer from Greek employer | 1-2 years, renewable |
| Digital Nomad Visa | Remote work, min income ~€3,500/month | 1 year, renewable |
| Financial Independence Visa | Proof of stable income (€2,000+/month) | 2 years, renewable |
| Student Visa | Enrollment at accredited institution | Duration of studies |
| Family Reunification | Family ties to Greek resident | Tied to sponsor's status |
| Golden Visa | €250,000+ real estate investment | 5 years, renewable |

## Digital Nomad / Remote Work
Greece introduced the **Digital Nomad Visa** (Law 4825/2021):
- For remote workers employed outside Greece
- Minimum income: ~€3,500/month (net)
- Valid for 1 year, renewable for up to 2 additional years
- Can apply at Greek consulates abroad
- Covers family members (spouse, children)
- Can lead to permanent residency after 5 years
- Fast-track processing (30-60 days)

### Financial Independence Visa
- For individuals with a stable income (€2,000+/month)
- No work permit — cannot work for a Greek employer
- Valid for 2 years, renewable
- Popular with retirees and remote workers who prefer not to use the DN visa

## Moving Checklist

### Before Moving
- [ ] **Passport**: Valid for duration of stay
- [ ] **Visa**: Apply 2-4 months in advance
- [ ] **Healthcare**: Register with EFKA (public insurance) or get private insurance
- [ ] **HRT**: Get 3-6 month supply and doctor's letter (translated to Greek)
- [ ] **Banking**: Consider opening a Greek bank account (National Bank, Piraeus, Alpha)
- [ ] **Housing**: Research rental market (spitogatos.gr, xe.gr)
- [ ] **Language**: Greek essential for bureaucracy; English in tourist areas
- [ ] **Document translation**: Official translations of key documents needed

### After Arrival
- [ ] Obtain AFM (tax number) from the tax office (ΔΟΥ)
- [ ] Register with the municipality (Δήμος)
- [ ] Apply for residence permit (non-EU)
- [ ] Register with EFKA (health insurance)
- [ ] Open local bank account
- [ ] Get local SIM card (Cosmote, Vodafone, Wind/Nova)
- [ ] Connect with LGBTQ+ organizations (SYNTHESI, Colour Youth)
- [ ] Find a GP and endocrinologist for HRT continuation

## Practical Information

### Cost of Living
- **Moderate** — lower than Western Europe
- Monthly rent varies by city:
  - Athens: €400-800
  - Thessaloniki: €300-600
  - Crete (Heraklion): €300-500
  - Islands: €400-700 (higher in tourist season)
- Monthly groceries: €250-400
- Dining out: €10-20 per meal

### Best Cities for LGBTQ+ Community
| City | LGBTQ+ Scene | Trans Healthcare |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Athens** | Good (Gazi district) | Best (Alexandras Hospital) |
| **Thessaloniki** | Moderate | Limited (AHEPA Hospital) |
| **Heraklion (Crete)** | Limited | Limited |
| **Patras** | Limited | Limited |

### Climate
- **Mediterranean**: Hot, dry summers (25-35°C)
- **Mild winters**: (5-15°C, rain)
- **Islands**: More temperate with sea breezes
- **Northern Greece**: Colder winters with snow possible

### Language
- Official language: Greek
- English: Widely spoken in tourist areas, less in rural regions
- Greek is essential for government bureaucracy
- Free Greek language courses available through the Ministry of Education
