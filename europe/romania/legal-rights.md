# Legal Rights in Romania

## Gender Recognition
Romania has a **medicalized gender recognition system** that functions but requires navigating multiple hurdles:

- **Requirements**: Psychiatric diagnosis + documented HRT + court decision
- **Process**: File a court case after obtaining medical certification
- **Surgery**: Not explicitly required by law, though some judges may inquire
- **Age**: 18+ only
- **Timeline**: Typically 6-18 months from diagnosis to final court decision

### The Process
1. Diagnosis from a psychiatrist or endocrinologist (Institutul de Endocrinologie in Bucharest)
2. Minimum 6-12 months of documented HRT
3. File a court case in your local tribunal
4. Medical expert testimony may be required
5. Court issues decision on gender marker change
6. Update civil registry and obtain new documents

**Note**: While the process is recognized, it is invasive, medicalizing, and varies by court in different counties. Courts in Bucharest, Cluj, and Timișoara are generally more favorable.

## Name Changes
- Processed through the **Civil Registry** (Starea Civilă)
- Can be done before gender marker change
- Requires justification — gender transition is accepted
- Processed within 30-60 days
- Some registrars may require additional documentation

## Anti-Discrimination Laws
- **Employment**: Prohibited under the Labour Code
- **Goods and Services**: Protected under Government Ordinance 137/2000 on combating discrimination
- **Education**: General protections apply
- **Healthcare**: Access to medical services protected

Romania's anti-discrimination framework is relatively comprehensive on paper, covering gender identity explicitly in some interpretations of the law. The **National Council for Combating Discrimination** (CNCD) handles complaints.

## Hate Crime Laws
- The **Criminal Code** includes hate crime provisions
- Sexual orientation is a protected characteristic
- Gender identity is **not explicitly included** but may be covered by extension
- Enforcement is inconsistent — police training on LGBTQ+ issues is limited
- Far-right groups are active, particularly in rural areas

## Document Recognition
- Updated passports and ID cards are recognized internationally
- Romanian IDs (CI) and passports can be updated after court decision
- Previous documents are archived
- Some trans people report difficulties with authorities who are unaware of the procedures
