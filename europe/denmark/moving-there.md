# Moving to Denmark

## Immigration Pathways

### EEA/EU Citizens
Free movement applies. Register with the International House or your local municipality within 3 months. You need a valid ID and proof of employment, study, or sufficient funds.

### UK Citizens
Post-Brexit, UK citizens must apply for a residence permit unless covered by the Withdrawal Agreement (resided in Denmark before 31 December 2020). The same rules as third-country nationals generally apply.

### Third-Country Nationals
- **Pay Limit Scheme**: For jobs with an annual salary above DKK 465,000 (2025). Fast processing (3-4 weeks).
- **Fast Track Scheme**: For pre-approved companies. Work permit tied to the employer.
- **Positive List**: For occupations experiencing a shortage (e.g., IT, engineering, healthcare).
- **Startup Denmark**: For entrepreneurs with an innovative, scalable business idea.
- **Family Reunification**: For spouses or registered partners.

## Digital Nomad / Remote Work

Denmark does not have a dedicated digital nomad visa. Remote workers typically use the Startup Denmark visa if they can establish a business, or the Pay Limit Scheme if employed by a Danish company. Freelancers may qualify under the self-employment route.

## Moving Checklist

### Before Moving
- [ ] Obtain necessary visa (non-EU)
- [ ] Apply for CPR (Central Person Register) number appointment
- [ ] Get health insurance (required until registered in Denmark)
- [ ] Research housing (Copenhagen is expensive; Aarhus, Odense cheaper)
- [ ] Gather medical records for HRT (translated to Danish or English)
- [ ] Notify your bank and redirect mail

### After Arrival
- [ ] Register at the International House / Citizen Service (within 3 months)
- [ ] Receive CPR number and Yellow Card (health insurance card)
- [ ] Apply for NemID/MitID (digital ID)
- [ ] Open a Danish bank account
- [ ] Find a GP (læge) — you can choose one after registration
- [ ] Apply for a Danish SIM card
- [ ] Connect with LGBT+ Denmark for community support

## Practical Information

### Cost of Living
- Monthly rent (1-bed): DKK 8,000-14,000 (Copenhagen), DKK 5,000-9,000 (other cities)
- Monthly budget (single person): DKK 10,000-15,000 excluding rent
- Public transport: DKK 300-600/month (Rejsekort)

### Climate
Temperate with mild summers (15-22°C) and cold winters (0-5°C). Dark winters with very short daylight (7 hours in December).

### Language
Danish is the official language. English is widely spoken — almost all Danes are fluent. Most official forms and healthcare are available in English. Learning Danish helps with integration but is not essential for daily life in cities.
