# Healthcare in Switzerland

## Gender Affirming Healthcare

Switzerland has a unique healthcare system where **major university hospitals** offer informed-consent-based trans care, while other regions may be more restrictive. The Swiss healthcare system is decentralized, with cantons responsible for healthcare delivery.

## HRT Access

### Informed Consent at Major Clinics

The following university hospitals operate an **informed consent model**:

| Clinic | Location | Services | Languages |
|--------|----------|----------|-----------|
| **University Hospital Zurich (USZ)** | Zurich | Full spectrum: HRT, surgery, psych | DE, EN, FR |
| **CHUV Lausanne** | Lausanne | HRT, surgical referrals | FR, EN, DE |
| **Inselspital Bern** | Bern | HRT, assessments | DE, EN, FR |
| **University Hospital Basel (USB)** | Basel | HRT, comprehensive care | DE, EN |
| **Hôpitaux Universitaires de Genève (HUG)** | Geneva | HRT, counselling | FR, EN, DE |

### Process at Informed Consent Clinics

1. **Initial Contact** — Self-referral or GP referral to the trans care unit
2. **Consultation** — Discussion of treatment goals, medical history
3. **Informed Consent** — Sign consent form after understanding risks and benefits
4. **Blood Tests** — Baseline hormone levels and health markers
5. **Prescription** — Hormones prescribed at the first or second appointment
6. **Follow-up** — Regular monitoring every 3-6 months

### Regional Gatekeeping

- In smaller cantons and rural areas, trans care may require:
  - Psychiatric diagnosis
  - Referral to endocrinology
  - Longer wait times
- Private psychiatrists and endocrinologists in smaller cities may not have trans care experience

## Prescription Portability

- **Switzerland is not in the EU/EEA** — EU prescription cross-border rules do not fully apply
- **EU prescriptions**: May be accepted but require Swiss doctor verification
- **Non-EU prescriptions**: Must be verified by a Swiss doctor
- **Importing medication**: Keep in original packaging with prescription label
- Carry a doctor's letter (English, German, or French) explaining the medication
- Swiss health insurance (basic coverage) covers trans-related care including HRT

## Insurance Coverage

Switzerland has mandatory **health insurance (Krankenversicherung / assurance maladie)**:

- Basic insurance covers gender-affirming treatments
- HRT medications are covered with a prescription
- Coverage for surgery requires a medical report and insurance approval
- Deductible (franchise) typically 300-2,500 CHF/year
- Co-payment (Selbstbehalt) is 10% up to 700 CHF/year

## Surgical Options

- Gender-affirming surgeries available at major university hospitals
- Referral from a recognized clinic required
- Insurance covers surgeries after medical necessity is established
- Wait times: 6-12 months for consultation, 6-18 months for surgery
- FFS (facial feminization surgery) may not be fully covered

## Mental Health Support

- Psychiatrists at university clinics
- **TGNS (Transgender Network Switzerland)** — Peer support groups
- **Pink Cross** — LGBTQ+ counselling
- Private therapists with trans experience (80-150 CHF/session)
