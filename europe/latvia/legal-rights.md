# Legal Rights in Latvia

## Gender Recognition
Latvia has one of the **most restrictive** gender recognition systems in the EU:

- **Requirements**: Diagnosis of gender dysphoria + sex reassignment surgery (sterilization implied)
- **Medical Assessment**: A multi-disciplinary medical commission evaluates applications
- **Age**: 18+ only
- **Process**: Application to the Medical Committee for Gender Change (under the Ministry of Health); surgery must be completed before legal gender change is approved
- **Surgery**: Legally required — no alternative pathway exists

### The Process
1. Referral to a psychiatrist specializing in gender dysphoria
2. Diagnosis from the medical commission
3. Mandatory real-life experience period
4. Sex reassignment surgery (must be completed)
5. Apply to the Civil Registry for gender marker change
6. Updated passport and ID card

**Note**: Latvian law effectively mandates sterilization. This has been criticized by international human rights bodies including the Council of Europe.

## Name Changes
- Name changes may be possible without full gender recognition
- Processed through the **Civil Registry** (Dzimtsarakstu nodaļa)
- Requires a valid reason; gender transition is generally accepted as valid
- Simpler than gender marker change but still bureaucratic

## Anti-Discrimination Laws
- **Employment**: Prohibited under the Labour Law (based on EU directives)
- **Goods and Services**: Protected under the Consumer Rights Protection Law
- **Education**: General equality provisions apply

The **Equal Treatment Law** prohibits discrimination based on sexual orientation but does not explicitly mention gender identity. Enforcement is weak, and few cases are brought.

## Hate Crime Laws
- Latvia does **not** have explicit hate crime legislation covering gender identity
- The Criminal Law includes enhanced penalties for crimes motivated by racial, national, or religious hatred
- Proposals to add sexual orientation and gender identity have been repeatedly rejected by parliament
- Some local advocacy organizations monitor transphobic incidents

## Document Recognition
- Updated passports reflect the new gender marker once the medical process is complete
- ID cards and driver's licenses can be updated accordingly
- Previous records remain in the system but are not publicly accessible
- EU travel documents are recognized within the Schengen Area
