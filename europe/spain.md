# Spain

## Quick Verdict
Spain offers excellent legal protections for transgender individuals, including self-determination gender recognition and comprehensive healthcare access.

## Immigration Pathways
- **EEA/EU Citizens**: Free movement rights - no visa required.
- **UK Citizens**: Visa-free for up to 90 days within 180 days (Schengen rule). For longer stays, apply for a residence permit.
- **Third-Country Nationals**: 
  - Work visa (requires job offer)
  - Student visa
  - Entrepreneur visa
  - Non-lucrative visa (for those with sufficient financial means)
  - Golden Visa (real estate investment)

## Legal Rights
- **Gender Recognition**: Law 3/2023 allows self-determination of gender from age 16+ (with parental consent for minors).
- **Name Changes**: Simple process through the Civil Registry.
- **Anti-Discrimination Laws**: Comprehensive protections based on gender identity.
- **Hate Crime Laws**: Gender identity is a protected characteristic.

## Healthcare & HRT
- **Informed Consent Model**: Available in many regions (Andalusia, Catalonia, Madrid, etc.) for hormone therapy.
- **Access**: Public healthcare covers hormone therapy and some surgeries. Private options available.
- **Prescription Portability**: EU prescriptions are valid across member states.
- **Clinics**: 
  - Public hospitals with gender units (e.g., Hospital Clínic Barcelona, Hospital Universitario La Paz Madrid)
  - Private specialized clinics

## Culture & Community
- **Organizations**:
  - FELGTB+ (State Federation of Lesbians, Gays, Trans, Bisexual, Intersex and more)
  - Chrysallis (association for families of trans minors)
  - Delta (trans youth organization)
- **Social Acceptance**: High in urban areas and regions like Madrid, Barcelona, Valencia. Some rural areas may be more conservative.
- **Events**: Pride events in major cities (Madrid Pride is one of the largest in Europe).
- **Language**: Spanish (Castilian) is official, but Catalan, Galician, and Basque are co-official in respective regions. English widely spoken in tourist areas.

## Quality of Life
- **Safety**: Generally safe, though petty crime can occur in tourist areas.
- **Healthcare System**: Universal healthcare (Sistema Nacional de Salud).
- **Cost of Living**: Moderate compared to other Western European countries (higher in major cities).
- **Climate**: Diverse - Mediterranean in south, oceanic in north, semi-arid in southeast.
- **Digital Infrastructure**: Good internet connectivity, growing remote work hub.
