# Moving to Croatia

## Immigration Pathways

### EU/EEA/Swiss Citizens

Croatia grants full **free movement rights** to EU/EEA/Swiss citizens:

- **Up to 3 months**: No registration required
- **Over 3 months**: Must register with the Ministry of Interior for a **boravak** (residence certificate)
- **Requirements**: Valid passport, proof of employment, sufficient funds, or health insurance
- **Right to work**: Automatic
- **After 5 years**: Eligible for permanent residence

### Non-EU Citizens

| Visa Type | Description | Requirements | Duration |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|----------|
| Work Permit | Employment with Croatian employer | Job offer, work permit application by employer | 1 year, renewable |
| EU Blue Card | Highly qualified workers | Degree + salary threshold (€40,000+/year) | 2 years, renewable |
| Student Visa | Full-time study | University acceptance, funds proof | Duration of studies |
| Family Reunification | Family of Croatian resident | Relationship proof, accommodation, income | 1 year, renewable |
| Business Visa | Self-employment | Business registration, sufficient funds | 1 year, renewable |

### Digital Nomad Visa

Croatia offers one of the best **Digital Nomad Visas** in Europe:

- Valid for **up to 12 months** (non-renewable)
- Must work for a company outside Croatia
- Income requirement: €2,500+/month (approximate)
- No Croatian tax liability
- Cannot be extended beyond 12 months
- Cannot switch to a regular work permit without leaving Croatia
- Allows bringing family members
- Popular option for remote workers

## Moving Checklist

### Before Moving
- [ ] **Passport**: Valid for at least 6 months
- [ ] **Visa/Residence**: Check requirements for your nationality
- [ ] **Health Insurance**: EU citizens: EHIC card; others: private insurance required
- [ ] **HRT**: Get 3-6 month supply and doctor's letter (English or Croatian)
- [ ] **Medical Records**: Translated into Croatian
- [ ] **Banking**: International banking options
- [ ] **Housing**: Research rental market (njuskalo.hr, crozilla.com)
- [ ] **Language**: Basic Croatian very helpful

### After Arrival
- [ ] **Register residence** with the local police (MUP) within 30 days
- [ ] **Apply for boravak** (residence certificate) for stays over 3 months
- [ ] **Get OIB** (personal identification number) — required for everything
- [ ] **Register with HZZO** (Croatian Health Insurance Fund)
- [ ] **Open bank account** — Zagrebačka banka, PBZ, Erste
- [ ] **Get Croatian phone number** — Hrvatski Telekom, A1, Telemach
- [ ] **Connect with TransAid Croatia** or similar organizations

## Practical Information

### Cost of Living
| Category | Zagreb | Split | Smaller Cities |
|----------|--------|-------|----------------|
| Rent (1-bedroom) | €400-700/month | €350-600/month | €250-450/month |
| Groceries | €200-350/month | €200-300/month | €180-280/month |
| Utilities | €100-200/month | €100-180/month | €80-150/month |
| Public transport | €40-60/month | €30-50/month | €20-40/month |
| Eating out | €8-15/meal | €8-15/meal | €6-12/meal |

### Language
- **Official language**: Croatian (South Slavic)
- **English**: Widely spoken among young people, especially in Zagreb and tourist areas
- **German**: Also relatively common
- **Integration**: Learning Croatian is important for long-term settlement
- Free Croatian courses available for refugees and some immigrants

### Climate
- **Mediterranean coast**: Hot summers, mild winters (Split, Dubrovnik, Rijeka)
- **Continental inland**: Cold winters, warm summers (Zagreb, Osijek)
- **Summer**: 25°C to 35°C (coast), 20°C to 30°C (inland)
- **Winter**: 5°C to 10°C (coast), -5°C to 5°C (inland)
- **Best time to arrive**: Spring or early autumn

### Trans-Specific Considerations
- **Zagreb is the most trans-friendly** city
- Rijeka and Split are moderately tolerant
- Rural areas and islands are very conservative
- Trans healthcare is very limited — plan for accessing care abroad
- **TransAid Croatia** provides community support
- Pride events in Zagreb, Rijeka, Split, and Osijek
- EU membership provides some legal protections
- Growing expat community, especially in Zagreb and along the coast
