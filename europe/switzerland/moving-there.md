# Moving to Switzerland

## Immigration Pathways

### EU/EFTA Citizens

Switzerland has **free movement agreements** with the EU and EFTA:

- **Up to 3 months**: No registration required
- **Over 3 months**: Must register with the cantonal migration office for a **B permit** (residence permit)
- **Requirements**: Valid passport, proof of employment, sufficient funds, or study enrollment
- **Right to work**: Automatic, no additional work permit needed
- **After 5 years**: Eligible for **C permit** (permanent residence)
- **After 10 years**: Eligible for Swiss citizenship

### Non-EU/EFTA Citizens

Switzerland operates a **strict quota system** for non-EU/EFTA nationals:

| Permit Type | Description | Requirements | Duration |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|----------|
| L Permit | Short-term work | Job offer, limited to 12 months | Up to 12 months |
| B Permit | Residence (work) | Job offer, priority given to EU/EFTA nationals | 1 year, renewable |
| B Permit (family) | Family reunification | Relationship to Swiss/EU resident | 1 year, renewable |
| Student Visa | Full-time study | University acceptance, funds proof (21,000+ CHF/year) | Duration of studies |
| Retirement Visa | Retirees | Sufficient funds (no work allowed) | 1 year, renewable |

**Key restrictions for non-EU:**
- Annual quotas limit the number of B and L permits for non-EU nationals
- Employer must prove no suitable Swiss/EU candidate exists
- Work permits tied to specific employer
- Highly skilled professionals (managers, specialists, researchers) prioritized
- Switching jobs requires new permit approval

### UK Citizens (Post-Brexit)

- UK-Switzerland **Migration and Mobility Agreement** (limited)
- UK citizens can enter visa-free for 90 days
- For longer stays: Apply through the cantonal migration office
- Work, study, and family reunification paths available
- UK citizens do not have free movement rights

## Digital Nomad / Remote Work

Switzerland does **not** have a specific digital nomad visa. Options for remote workers:

- **B Permit with remote employment**: Possible if the employer has a Swiss presence or through a Swiss payroll company
- **L Permit**: Short-term options limited
- Some cantons are more flexible than others
- B permit requires a residence in Switzerland
- **Border crossing**: Remote workers from neighbouring countries can live in nearby EU countries and commute

## Moving Checklist

### Before Moving
- [ ] **Passport**: Valid for at least 6 months
- [ ] **Visa/Residence**: Check requirements based on nationality
- [ ] **Health Insurance**: Mandatory within 3 months of arrival (compare on comparis.ch)
- [ ] **HRT**: Get 3-6 month supply and doctor's letter (English, German, or French)
- [ ] **Medical Records**: Translated into German or French
- [ ] **Banking**: International banking options
- [ ] **Housing**: Research rental market (comparis.ch, homegate.ch)
- [ ] **Language**: Basic German, French, or Italian very helpful

### After Arrival
- [ ] Register with the cantonal **Einwohnerkontrolle** (residents' registration office)
- [ ] Apply for residence permit (B permit for EU/EFTA citizens)
- [ ] Get health insurance within 3 months
- [ ] Open a Swiss bank account (PostFinance, UBS, Credit Suisse)
- [ ] Register with a GP
- [ ] Get a Swiss phone number (Swisscom, Sunrise, Salt)
- [ ] Connect with TGNS or local LGBTQ+ groups

## Practical Information

### Cost of Living
| Category | Cost (CHF) |
|----------|-------------|
| Rent (1-bedroom, Zurich/Geneva) | 1,500-2,500 CHF/month |
| Rent (1-bedroom, smaller cities) | 1,000-1,500 CHF/month |
| Groceries | 400-600 CHF/month |
| Health insurance | 200-500 CHF/month (basic) |
| Public transport pass | 80-150 CHF/month |
| Mobile phone plan | 30-60 CHF/month |

### Languages
- **Official languages**: German (63%), French (23%), Italian (8%), Romansh (1%)
- **English**: Widely spoken in business and major cities, especially Zurich and Geneva
- **Integration**: Learning the local language is important for long-term settlement
- Cantonal language determines which language to learn

### Climate
- **Alpine climate**: Cold winters, mild summers
- **Winter**: -5°C to 5°C (December-February)
- **Summer**: 15°C to 25°C (June-August)
- **Best time to arrive**: Spring or early autumn

### Trans-Specific Considerations
- **Switzerland is generally very safe** for trans people
- Zurich, Geneva, Basel, and Bern have the most accepting environments
- Rural and conservative cantons may be less welcoming
- Healthcare access is best in major cities
- TGNS provides community support and resources
- LGBTQ+ events and spaces are well-established in larger cities
