# Moving to Germany

## Immigration Pathways

### EEA/EU Citizens
Free movement applies. Register your residence at the Bürgeramt (citizen's office) within 14 days of moving into an apartment. No visa required. EU citizens get a Freizügigkeitsbescheinigung (freedom of movement certificate).

### UK Citizens
Post-Brexit, UK citizens need a residence permit. Those who resided in Germany before 31 December 2020 are protected under the Withdrawal Agreement. Others must apply through standard third-country routes.

### Third-Country Nationals
- **Work Visa (Aufenthaltserlaubnis)**: Requires a job offer. The Federal Employment Agency may check if the position could be filled by an EU citizen.
- **EU Blue Card**: For highly skilled workers with a salary threshold (~€43,800/year for shortage occupations, ~€58,400/year for others in 2025).
- **Freelancer Visa (Freiberufler)**: Available for self-employed professionals (artists, writers, IT consultants). Requires proof of clients and income.
- **Job Seeker Visa**: 6-month visa to find employment in Germany.
- **Student Visa**: For university studies. Part-time work permitted (120 full or 240 half days/year).

## Digital Nomad / Remote Work

Germany does not have a dedicated digital nomad visa. However, the **Freiberufler visa** is an excellent option for self-employed remote workers in creative and professional fields. You need to demonstrate sufficient income, health insurance, and a clear business plan. The **Work Visa (ICT)** may suit employees of multinational companies.

## Moving Checklist

### Before Moving
- [ ] Obtain necessary visa (non-EU)
- [ ] Arrange health insurance (public or private — mandatory)
- [ ] Research housing (Berlin, Hamburg, Munich, Cologne, Leipzig)
- [ ] Gather medical records and HRT documentation (translated to German)
- [ ] Learn basic German (helpful for bureaucracy)
- [ ] Notify your bank and redirect mail

### After Arrival
- [ ] Register your address at the Bürgeramt (within 14 days)
- [ ] Apply for residence permit (non-EU)
- [ ] Open a German bank account (Girokonto)
- [ ] Get health insurance confirmation and card
- [ ] Find a GP (Hausarzt) — use Queermed for trans-friendly listings
- [ ] Apply for a German SIM card (Telekom, Vodafone, O2)
- [ ] Apply for a Tax ID (Steuer-ID) — usually automatic after registration
- [ ] Connect with Bundesverband Trans or local LGBTQ+ groups

## Practical Information

### Cost of Living
- Monthly rent (1-bed): €600-1,200 (Berlin), €800-1,600 (Munich), €500-900 (Leipzig)
- Monthly budget (single person): €800-1,200 excluding rent
- Public transport: €50-100/month (Deutschlandticket €58/month for nationwide travel)

### Climate
Temperate seasonal climate. Summers 20-30°C, winters -5 to 5°C. Southern Germany has colder winters with snow; western regions milder.

### Language
German is the official language. English proficiency is high in major cities, tech industry, and academia. Bureaucracy is German-only — bring a translator or learn the language. Integration courses are available.
