# Moving to Norway

## Immigration Pathways

### EU/EEA/Swiss Citizens
Norway is not an EU member but is part of the **EEA (European Economic Area)** and the **Schengen Area**. This grants EU/EEA/Swiss citizens free movement rights, including the right to live, work, and study in Norway. Registration with the police (politiet) is required for stays over three months.

### Non-EU Citizens

| Visa Type | Description | Requirements | Duration |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|----------|
| Skilled Worker Visa | For qualified professionals with job offer | Degree + job offer, salary at least ~€45,000/year | 1-3 years, renewable |
| Job Seeker Visa | For searching for skilled work | Master's degree or PhD, sufficient funds | 6 months |
| Family Immigration | For family of Norwegian residents | Relationship proof, sufficient income (€35,000+/year) | 1 year, renewable |
| Student Visa | For full-time study | University acceptance, funds proof (€14,000+/year) | 1 year, renewable |
| Protection (Asylum) | For those with protection needs | Refugee status or humanitarian grounds | 1-3 years |

## Key Relocation Steps

1. **Find accommodation** — Housing market is very tight, especially in Oslo
2. **Register with the police** — Within 3 months of arrival (EU/EEA) or before arrival (non-EU)
3. **Get a Norwegian ID number (fødselsnummer)** — Essential for all services
4. **Register with a GP (fastlege)** — Every resident is entitled to a GP
5. **Open a bank account** — Requires Norwegian ID number
6. **Apply for health insurance** — EU/EEA citizens use EHIC; non-EU register with NAV
7. **Learn Norwegian** — Essential for integration and citizenship (B1 required)

## Cost of Living

Norway has one of the highest costs of living in the world:
- Rent (1-bedroom): €1,200-1,800/month (Oslo); €800-1,200 (other cities)
- Groceries: €400-600/month
- Public transport: €80-120/month pass
- Health insurance: Public system, annual deductible cap ~€260
- Eating out: €25-40 for a main course

## Trans-Specific Relocation Tips

- **Norway is extremely safe** — very low crime rates and high social acceptance
- **Healthcare is centralised** — most trans care goes through Oslo
- **English proficiency is very high** — nearly all Norwegians speak fluent English
- **Bring medical records translated into Norwegian or English**
- **Carry at least 3 months of HRT medication** with a doctor's letter
- **Register with a GP (fastlege)** immediately — they can refer to NBTS
- **Consider living in Oslo** for best access to trans healthcare
- **Join FRI** for community support — they have local chapters across the country
- **The climate can be challenging** — long, dark winters, especially above the Arctic Circle

## Language

- **Official language**: Norwegian (Bokmål and Nynorsk)
- **English** — Very widely spoken, over 90% fluency
- **Norwegian** required for permanent residence (A2) and citizenship (B1)
- Free Norwegian language courses are available through municipalities
- Many universities offer Norwegian language programmes
