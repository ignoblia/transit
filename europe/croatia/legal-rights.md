# Legal Rights in Croatia

## Gender Recognition

Croatia operates a **medicalized model** for legal gender recognition. The process requires medical intervention and court approval.

### Requirements

- Psychiatric diagnosis of gender dysphoria
- **Minimum 1 year** of hormone replacement therapy (HRT)
- Court application with medical evidence
- Psychological evaluation reports
- No explicit sterilization requirement (removed in practice)
- Minimum age: 18

### Process

1. Initial psychiatric assessment and diagnosis (3-6 months)
2. Begin HRT (requires endocrinologist involvement)
3. After 1 year of HRT, collect comprehensive medical reports
4. Submit application to the municipal court
5. Court hearing with medical expert testimony
6. Court decision typically takes 3-6 months
7. Register the change with the civil registry (Matica)

## Name Changes

- Name changes can be requested alongside gender marker change
- Separate name-only changes require a justified reason
- For trans people, the name change requires medical documentation in practice
- Processed through the civil registry office

## Anti-Discrimination Laws

Croatia has moderate anti-discrimination protections:

- **Anti-Discrimination Act (2008)** — Prohibits discrimination based on gender identity
- Gender identity is explicitly listed as a protected ground
- **Employment** — Protection in hiring, promotion, dismissal
- **Education** — Protection in schools and universities
- **Goods and Services** — Access to services
- **Housing** — Protection in rental and purchase
- Enforcement is inconsistent, with significant variation between urban and rural areas

## Hate Crime Laws

- Croatia's Criminal Code includes hate crime provisions
- Gender identity is explicitly included as a protected characteristic
- Enhanced penalties for crimes motivated by transphobia
- Reporting rates are low due to distrust of police
- Prosecution rates are moderate
- LGBTQ+ organizations note inconsistent application of the law

## Document Recognition

- Updated gender markers on Croatian passports and IDs are recognized
- Croatian passports follow EU standards
- Third gender / "X" marker not available
- Updated documents accepted within the EU

## Social Context

- Croatia is socially conservative, especially in rural areas
- Zagreb, Rijeka, and Split are more tolerant
- Pride events have grown significantly in size and acceptance
- EU membership has driven legal improvements
- LGBTQ+ rights remain a contentious political issue

## Summary

| Aspect | Status |
|--------|--------|
| Gender Recognition | Medicalized (diagnosis + 1 year HRT + court) |
| Surgery Required | No (removed in practice) |
| Third Gender | Not available |
| Anti-Discrimination | Moderate (explicitly includes gender identity) |
| Hate Crime Protections | Moderate |
