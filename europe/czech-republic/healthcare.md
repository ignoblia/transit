# Healthcare in the Czech Republic

## Gender Affirming Healthcare

The Czech Republic operates a **gatekeeping model** centralised at the **Sexuologický ústav** (Institute of Sexology) in Prague. This institute has historically been the gatekeeper for all trans healthcare in the country, though some decentralization has occurred.

## HRT Access

### Requirements

- Psychiatric diagnosis of gender dysphoria at an authorized sexology clinic
- Referral to an endocrinologist
- Regular psychological follow-up
- Minimum assessment period

### Process

1. **Referral** — GP referral to a sexology clinic
2. **Assessment** — Multiple sessions at the Sexuologický ústav (typically 3-6 months)
3. **Diagnosis** — Gender dysphoria diagnosis
4. **Endocrinology Referral** — HRT initiation after diagnosis
5. **Hormone Therapy** — Prescribed and monitored by endocrinologist
6. **Ongoing Monitoring** — Regular follow-up with both sexologist and endocrinologist

### Key Clinics

| Clinic | Location | Services | Wait Times |
|--------|----------|----------|------------|
| Sexuologický ústav (VFN) | Prague | Full spectrum (central authority) | 3-6 months |
| Fakultní nemocnice Brno | Brno | Diagnosis, HRT | 3-6 months |
| Fakultní nemocnice Ostrava | Ostrava | Diagnosis, HRT | 4-8 months |
| Fakultní nemocnice Plzeň | Plzeň | Limited services | 4-8 months |
| Private sexologists | Prague/Brno | Shorter waits, costs apply | 1-3 months |

### Challenges

- Diagnostic process is pathologizing and intrusive
- The sterilization requirement (for legal gender change) also affects healthcare decisions
- Very few providers outside Prague
- Long waiting times for public clinics
- Language barrier (most providers speak Czech only)
- Outdated diagnostic approaches at some clinics

## Prescription Portability

- **EU member** — EU prescription cross-border rules apply
- EU/EEA prescriptions valid for up to 30 days
- Czech Health Insurance (VZP) covers prescriptions
- Non-EU prescriptions require verification
- HRT medications generally available at pharmacies
- Generic versions of oestradiol and testosterone widely available

## Insurance Coverage

The Czech Republic has **mandatory public health insurance (VZP and others)** :

- Full coverage for trans-related care including:
  - Psychiatric assessments
  - Endocrinology
  - HRT medications
  - Gender-affirming surgeries (after approval)
- Prescription fee: ~30 CZK per item
- No co-payment for covered services

## Surgical Options

- Gender-affirming surgeries are covered by public insurance
- **Top surgery (mammoplasty)** : Covered, wait 6-12 months
- **Bottom surgery** : Available, wait 12-24 months
- **Hysterectomy/oophorectomy** : Covered (required for legal recognition)
- **FFS**: Not covered by insurance
- Surgery is performed at motol University Hospital in Prague and other facilities
- Quality is variable — some people travel abroad for better outcomes

## Mental Health Support

- **Sexuologický ústav** — Psychological support during assessment
- **Trans*parent** — Peer support and advocacy organization
- **Prague Pride** — Community events and support
- **Platforma pro rovnoprávnost** — Advocacy and counselling
- Private psychologists: 600-1,200 CZK/session

## Costs

| Service | Typical Cost | Insurance Coverage |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| GP visit | Free | Full |
| Sexology assessment | Free | Full |
| Endocrinology consult | Free | Full |
| HRT (monthly) | 30-150 CZK | Prescription fee only |
| Blood tests | Free | Full |
| Private psychologist | 600-1,200 CZK | Not covered |
