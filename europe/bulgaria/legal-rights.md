# Legal Rights in Bulgaria

## Gender Recognition
Bulgaria has **no clear legal framework** for gender recognition — it relies entirely on inconsistent court decisions:

- **Requirements**: Psychiatric diagnosis of gender dysphoria is generally required, but courts have varied opinions
- **No statutory procedure**: There is no dedicated law governing gender recognition
- **Process**: Must file a court case — outcomes vary significantly by judge and region
- **Surgery**: Not explicitly required by law, but some judges demand it
- **Age**: 18+ only
- **Outcome**: Many cases are rejected; successful applicants report a years-long process

### The Process
1. Diagnosis from a psychiatrist
2. Gather medical documentation
3. File a court case for gender marker change
4. Attend court hearings (may require multiple appearances)
5. If approved, update civil registry documents

**Note**: The lack of a clear legal framework means every case is a legal battle. The European Court of Human Rights has ruled against Bulgaria for failing to provide clear gender recognition procedures, but the government has not implemented reforms.

## Name Changes
- Processed through the **Civil Registry** (Гражданска регистрация)
- Requires justification — gender transition is accepted as a valid reason
- Name change can sometimes be done separately from gender marker change
- Processed within 1-3 months
- Court involvement may be required if the registry refuses

## Anti-Discrimination Laws
- **Employment**: Prohibited under the Labour Code, but gender identity is not explicitly protected
- **Goods and Services**: General non-discrimination provisions under the Protection Against Discrimination Act
- **Education**: Limited protections
- **Enforcement**: The Commission for Protection Against Discrimination handles complaints but is not always effective

The **Protection Against Discrimination Act** prohibits discrimination based on "sexual orientation" but does not explicitly include gender identity. Courts have inconsistently applied protections.

## Hate Crime Laws
- The **Criminal Code** does not explicitly include gender identity in hate crime provisions
- Sexual orientation is not explicitly covered either
- No enhanced penalties for anti-trans hate crimes
- Bulgaria has been criticized by the EU for failing to protect LGBTQ+ people from hate crimes

## Document Recognition
- Updated documents are recognized internationally in principle
- Many trans people report difficulties with border control and administrative authorities
- Previous documents may be requested by authorities
- Bulgarian IDs and passports reflect the gender marker from civil registry
