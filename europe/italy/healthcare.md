# Healthcare & HRT in Italy

## Gatekeeping Model
Italy operates a **gatekeeping model** for transgender healthcare:

- **Psychiatric diagnosis required** to start HRT
- Diagnosis followed by a minimum 1-2 year real-life experience and HRT period before surgery
- Treatment centralized at a small number of public gender clinics
- Northern regions have better access than southern regions

## Accessing HRT

### Step 1: Find a Gender Clinic
Contact one of the authorized Gender Identity Centers (Centri di Identità di Genere):
- **Careggi University Hospital** — Florence (largest, most experienced)
- **San Camillo-Forlanini Hospital** — Rome
- **San Raffaele Hospital** — Milan
- **Sant'Andrea Hospital** — Rome
- **Sant'Orsola-Malpighi Hospital** — Bologna

### Step 2: Psychological Assessment
- 3-6 sessions with a psychologist/psychiatrist at the clinic
- Diagnosis of gender dysphoria
- Discussion of treatment goals and timeline

### Step 3: Hormone Therapy
- Endocrinologist prescribes HRT after diagnosis
- Baseline blood tests required
- SSN (Servizio Sanitario Nazionale) covers HRT costs
- Follow-up every 3-6 months

## Prescription Portability
- **EU prescriptions**: Valid in Italy under EU mutual recognition
- **Non-EU prescriptions**: Must be verified by an Italian doctor; best to bring detailed medical history
- **Importing medication**: 3-month supply for personal use with doctor's letter
- **Generic names**: Know estradiol valerate, cyproterone acetate, spironolactone, testosterone gel
- **Pharmacies**: Farmacie are widely available; some may need to order specific medications

## Surgical Options
- Gender-affirming surgeries are **covered by public healthcare** with authorization
- Authorization requires: diagnosis + minimum 1-2 years HRT + court approval (for legal change)
- Major surgical centers: Careggi (Florence), San Camillo (Rome), San Raffaele (Milan)
- Public wait times: 12-24 months for consultation, 18-36 months for surgery
- Private surgery: €10,000-30,000
- Facial feminization surgery is generally not covered by public healthcare

## Costs

| Service | Public (SSN) | Private |
|---------|--------------|---------|
| Psychiatric assessment | Free (public) | €100-200 |
| Endocrinology visit | Free (public) | €80-150 |
| HRT (monthly) | €10-30 | €40-80 |
| Top surgery | Covered (public) | €5,000-10,000 |
| Bottom surgery | Covered (public) | €15,000-30,000 |
