# Moving to Bulgaria

## Immigration Pathways

### EEA/EU Citizens
- **Free movement rights** — no visa required
- **Up to 3 months**: No registration needed
- **Over 3 months**: Register with local Migration Directorate
- **Right to work**: Automatic
- **Residence registration**: Required at the municipality (адресна регистрация)

### UK Citizens (Post-Brexit)
- **Schengen Area**: Visa-free for up to 90 days in any 180-day period
- **Long-term residency**: Requires a residence permit:
  - Work permit (job offer from Bulgarian employer)
  - Student visa (enrollment at Bulgarian university)
  - Family reunification
  - Long-term residency permit

### Third-Country Nationals
| Visa Type | Requirements | Duration |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| Work Permit | Job offer from Bulgarian employer | Up to 1 year, renewable |
| Student Visa | Enrollment at accredited institution | Duration of studies |
| Family Reunification | Family ties to Bulgarian resident | Tied to sponsor's status |
| EU Blue Card | Highly skilled (salary ~€30,000+/year) | Up to 4 years |
| Type D Visa | Long-stay visa for various purposes | Up to 1 year |

## Digital Nomad / Remote Work
Bulgaria does **not have a dedicated digital nomad visa**. Options:
- **Type D visa** for long-stay (up to 1 year) — requires purpose justification
- **Work visa** if employed by a Bulgarian company
- **Tourist visa** for up to 90 days (no work permitted)

## Moving Checklist

### Before Moving
- [ ] **Passport**: Valid for duration of stay
- [ ] **Visa**: Apply 2-3 months in advance (non-EU)
- [ ] **Healthcare**: Private health insurance initially; register with NHIF after residency
- [ ] **HRT**: Get 3-6 month supply and doctor's letter (translated to Bulgarian)
- [ ] **Banking**: Consider UniCredit Bulbank, DSK, or Revolut
- [ ] **Housing**: Research rental market (imot.bg, alo.bg)
- [ ] **Language**: Bulgarian essential for daily life
- [ ] **Document translation**: Official Bulgarian translations needed

### After Arrival
- [ ] Register with Migration Directorate (non-EU) or municipality (EU)
- [ ] Obtain Bulgarian personal number (ЕГН) — essential for all services
- [ ] Register with NHIF (Национална здравноосигурителна каса)
- [ ] Open local bank account
- [ ] Get local SIM card (Vivacom, A1, Telenor)
- [ ] Find LGBTQ+ community connections (very limited)

## Practical Information

### Cost of Living
- **Low** — one of the cheapest EU countries
- Monthly rent (1-bed): €250-500 (Sofia), €200-400 (Plovdiv), €150-300 (Varna)
- Monthly groceries: €200-300
- Public transport: €25/month (Sofia)
- Dining out: €5-10 per meal

### Best Cities
| City | LGBTQ+ Scene | Trans Healthcare |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Sofia** | Very limited, underground | Best option (still very poor) |
| **Plovdiv** | Minimal | None |
| **Varna** | Minimal | None |

### Climate
- **Winters**: Cold (-5°C to 5°C), snow common
- **Summers**: Warm to hot (25-35°C)
- **Best time to arrive**: Spring or early autumn

### Language
- Official language: Bulgarian (Cyrillic alphabet)
- English: Limited outside Sofia and tourist areas
- Russian: Somewhat understood by older generations
- Bulgarian is essential for daily life and bureaucracy
