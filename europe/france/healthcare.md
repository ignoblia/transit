# Healthcare & HRT in France

## Access Model

France operates broadly on an informed consent model. Since 2010, the Haute Autorité de Santé (HAS) removed the requirement for a psychiatric diagnosis to access transgender healthcare. In practice, a GP or endocrinologist can prescribe HRT under an informed consent framework, though some doctors still prefer a letter from a psychologist.

Public health insurance (Sécurité Sociale) covers transgender healthcare. ALD (Affection Longue Durée) status is available for trans-related care, providing 100% reimbursement for many treatments.

## Prescription Portability

As an EU member, France recognises prescriptions from other EU/EEA countries. Non-EU immigrants must see a French doctor for a local prescription. A medical summary in French documenting your HRT history is highly recommended.

A 3-month supply of HRT medications for personal use is generally permitted with a valid prescription.

## Surgical Options

Gender-affirming surgeries are covered by Sécurité Sociale under ALD status, including top surgery, bottom surgery, hysterectomy, and some facial feminisation procedures. Major surgical centres are in Paris, Lyon, and Marseille. Wait times are 6-18 months for most procedures.
