# Legal Rights in Spain

## Gender Recognition
The **Law 3/2023** (Ley Trans) allows self-determination of gender:

- **Age**: 16+ can self-declare; 12-16 with judicial approval; under 12 requires court order
- **Medical Requirements**: None — no diagnosis, surgery, or hormone therapy required
- **Process**: Simple administrative declaration at Civil Registry (Registro Civil)
- **Timeframe**: 3-4 months typically

## Name Changes
- Simple process through the **Civil Registry**
- No medical requirements
- Processed alongside gender marker change or separately
- Usually takes 2-4 months

## Anti-Discrimination Laws
Spain has comprehensive protections at both national and regional levels:
- **Employment**: Protected under national labor law
- **Housing**: Explicit protections in housing legislation
- **Education**: Schools must respect gender identity
- **Healthcare**: Access to trans healthcare is a legal right
- **Public Services**: Cannot be denied based on gender identity

## Regional Variations
Some autonomous communities have additional protections:
- **Catalonia**: Comprehensive LGBT+ law (Law 11/2014)
- **Madrid**: Trans-specific healthcare protocols
- **Andalusia**: Pioneering trans healthcare since 1999
- **Basque Country**: Strong anti-discrimination legislation
- **Valencia**: Comprehensive trans rights law

## Hate Crime Laws
Gender identity is explicitly included as a protected characteristic in the Criminal Code.
