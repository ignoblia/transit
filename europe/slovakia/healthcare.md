# Healthcare & HRT in Slovakia

## Gatekeeping Model
Slovakia's transgender healthcare is **limited to Bratislava** with very few providers:

- **Psychiatric diagnosis required** for access to HRT
- Most services concentrated in **Bratislava** at major hospitals
- Very few providers have experience with trans patients
- Long wait times (6-12 months for initial appointment)

## Accessing HRT

### Step 1: Psychiatric Assessment
- Contact **Psychiatric Clinic of the Medical Faculty of Comenius University** in Bratislava
- Also possible at the **Sexuology Department** of the same institution
- Diagnosis of gender dysphoria per ICD-10/11
- Multiple sessions over 3-6 months
- Very limited availability outside Bratislava

### Step 2: Endocrinology Referral
- Referral to an endocrinologist for hormone therapy
- Baseline blood tests required
- HRT prescribed and monitored
- Follow-up visits every 3-6 months

### Step 3: Ongoing Care
- Regular monitoring by endocrinologist
- Some GPs may manage ongoing HRT after specialist establishes treatment
- Medications generally available at pharmacies

## Prescription Portability
- **EU prescriptions**: Valid in Slovakia under EU mutual recognition
- **Non-EU prescriptions**: Must be re-evaluated by a Slovak doctor
- **Importing medication**: 3-month supply permitted with a doctor's letter
- **Medication availability**: Generally good in major cities
- **Pharmacies**: Major chains (Dr.Max, BENU) nationwide

## Surgical Options
- **No gender-affirming surgeries available** in Slovakia domestically
- The legal requirement for sterilization means surgery is required for legal recognition
- Surgery must be sought abroad (Thailand, Germany, Serbia, Belgium)
- No public funding for gender-affirming surgery
- Previous providers in Slovakia have discontinued services
