# Legal Rights in Finland

## Gender Recognition

Finland passed a landmark self-ID law that took effect in 2023, replacing the previous requirement for sterilisation or medical diagnosis. Adults can now change their legal gender by simply filing a declaration with the Digital and Population Data Services Agency. A short reflection period of 30 days applies, after which the change must be confirmed.

For minors aged 15-17, parental consent is required. Those under 15 need a doctor's statement confirming their gender identity is stable.

## Name Changes

Name changes can be submitted alongside your gender recognition application through the Digital and Population Data Services Agency. There is a small administrative fee (approximately €50). The process takes 2-4 weeks.

## Anti-Discrimination Laws

Finland prohibits discrimination based on gender identity in:

- **Employment**: Recruitment, working conditions, termination
- **Goods and services**: Healthcare, housing, banking
- **Education**: From primary to university
- **Social benefits**: Access to welfare and pensions

The Non-Discrimination Ombudsman handles complaints and investigations.

## Hate Crime Laws

Gender identity is explicitly recognised as a hate crime motivation under Finnish law. Courts can impose enhanced penalties for crimes motivated by transphobia. The police have specialised hate crime investigation units.
