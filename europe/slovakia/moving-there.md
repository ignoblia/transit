# Moving to Slovakia

## Immigration Pathways

### EEA/EU Citizens
- **Free movement rights** — no visa required
- **Up to 3 months**: No registration needed
- **Over 3 months**: Register with the Foreign Police (Cudzinecká polícia)
- **Right to work**: Automatic
- **Residence registration**: Required at the municipality

### UK Citizens (Post-Brexit)
- **Schengen Area**: Visa-free for up to 90 days in any 180-day period
- **Long-term residency**: Requires a residence permit:
  - Work permit (job offer from Slovak employer)
  - Student visa (enrollment at Slovak university)
  - Family reunification
  - Slovak Startup Visa

### Third-Country Nationals
| Visa Type | Requirements | Duration |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| Work Permit | Job offer from Slovak employer | Up to 2 years, renewable |
| Student Visa | Enrollment at accredited institution | Duration of studies |
| Family Reunification | Family ties to Slovak resident | Tied to sponsor's status |
| EU Blue Card | Highly skilled (salary ~€30,000+/year) | Up to 4 years |
| Startup Visa | Innovative business, approved by Slovak Investment Agency | Up to 2 years |

## Digital Nomad / Remote Work
Slovakia does **not have a dedicated digital nomad visa**. Options:
- **Work visa** if employed by a Slovak company
- **Tourist visa** for up to 90 days (no work permitted)
- **Startup visa** for entrepreneurs
- Some remote workers use the **short-term work visa** for specific projects

## Moving Checklist

### Before Moving
- [ ] **Passport**: Valid for duration of stay
- [ ] **Visa**: Apply 2-3 months in advance (non-EU)
- [ ] **Healthcare**: Private health insurance initially; register with VšZP after residency
- [ ] **HRT**: Get 6-month supply and doctor's letter (translated to Slovak)
- [ ] **Banking**: Consider Slovenská sporiteľňa, Tatra banka, or Revolut
- [ ] **Housing**: Research rental market (nehnutelnosti.sk, topreality.sk)
- [ ] **Language**: Slovak essential for daily life
- [ ] **Document translation**: Official Slovak translations needed

### After Arrival
- [ ] Register with Foreign Police (non-EU) or municipality (EU)
- [ ] Obtain Slovak ID number (Rodné číslo) — essential for all services
- [ ] Register with VšZP (General Health Insurance Company)
- [ ] Open local bank account
- [ ] Get local SIM card (Orange, Telekom, O2)
- [ ] Connect with LGBTQ+ community (Iniciatíva Inakosť, Proti Prúdu)
- [ ] Register with Bratislava psychiatric clinic for trans healthcare

## Practical Information

### Cost of Living
- **Low to moderate** — affordable EU destination
- Monthly rent (1-bed): €400-750 (Bratislava), €300-500 (Košice), €250-400 (Žilina)
- Monthly groceries: €200-350
- Public transport: €25-35/month (Bratislava)
- Dining out: €7-14 per meal

### Best Cities for LGBTQ+ Community
| City | LGBTQ+ Scene | Trans Healthcare |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Bratislava** | Small but active | Only real option |
| **Košice** | Very limited | None |
| **Žilina** | Minimal | None |

### Climate
- **Winters**: Cold (-5°C to -10°C), snow common in mountains
- **Summers**: Warm (20-30°C)
- **Best time to arrive**: Spring or early autumn

### Language
- Official language: Slovak (West Slavic, similar to Czech)
- English: Moderate in Bratislava, limited elsewhere
- Czech: Widely understood due to historical ties
- German: Somewhat understood in western areas
- Slovak is essential for daily life and bureaucracy
