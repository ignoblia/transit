# Healthcare & HRT in Finland

## Access Model

Finland has historically followed a gatekeeping model for transgender healthcare, requiring a psychiatric diagnosis of gender dysphoria before accessing HRT. The system is in transition following the 2023 self-ID law, but clinical practice has been slower to change. Access is centralised through five university hospital gender clinics.

The typical pathway involves: GP referral → psychiatric evaluation (6-12 month wait) → diagnosis → endocrinology referral → HRT. Total wait time from first appointment to HRT can be 1-2 years.

## Key Clinics

| Clinic | Location | Services |
|--------|----------|----------|
| HUS Gender Clinic | Helsinki | Transdiagnostic assessment, HRT, surgery referrals |
| TAYS Gender Clinic | Tampere | Gender identity evaluation, HRT |
| TYKS Gender Clinic | Turku | Assessment, HRT, counselling |
| OYS Gender Clinic | Oulu | Gender services, HRT |
| KYS Gender Clinic | Kuopio | Gender identity services |

## Prescription Portability

Finland recognises EU/EEA prescriptions. Non-EU arrivals must see a Finnish doctor to obtain local prescriptions. A translated medical history (in Finnish, Swedish, or English) documenting your HRT is strongly recommended.

Carry up to a 3-month supply of HRT for personal use with a valid prescription from your home country.

## Surgical Options

Gender-affirming surgeries are available at HUS (Helsinki) and are publicly funded for residents. Top surgery, bottom surgery, hysterectomy, and facial feminisation are available. Wait times for surgery are typically 12-24 months. Some patients choose private surgery abroad (e.g., Thailand, Germany).
