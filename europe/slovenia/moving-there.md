# Moving to Slovenia

## Immigration Pathways

### EEA/EU Citizens
- **Free movement rights** — no visa required
- **Up to 3 months**: No formalities
- **Over 3 months**: Register with the Administrative Unit (Upravna enota) and obtain a residence certificate
- **Right to work**: Automatic
- **EMŠO (Personal ID number)**: Required for all official services

### UK Citizens (Post-Brexit)
- **Schengen Area**: Visa-free for up to 90 days in any 180-day period
- **Longer stays**: Apply for residence permit:
  - Work permit (job offer from Slovenian employer)
  - Student visa (enrollment at Slovenian university)
  - Family reunification
  - Temporary residence for business purposes

### Third-Country Nationals
| Visa Type | Requirements | Duration |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| Work Permit | Job offer from Slovenian employer | Up to 1 year, renewable |
| EU Blue Card | Highly skilled (salary ~€35,000+/year) | Up to 4 years |
| Student Visa | Enrollment at accredited institution | Duration of studies |
| Family Reunification | Family ties to Slovenian resident | Tied to sponsor's status |
| Business Visa | Self-employment or business activity | Up to 1 year, renewable |
| Temporary Residence | Various grounds (study, work, family) | 1 year, renewable |

## Digital Nomad / Remote Work
Slovenia does **not have a dedicated digital nomad visa**. Remote workers can consider:
- **Temporary residence permit for business**: For self-employed individuals with a registered business
- **Work permit**: If employed by a Slovenian company
- **Tourist visa**: Up to 90 days (no work permitted)
- Some remote workers use the **EU Blue Card** route if employed by a tech company

## Moving Checklist

### Before Moving
- [ ] **Passport**: Valid for duration of stay
- [ ] **Visa**: Apply 2-3 months in advance
- [ ] **Healthcare**: Private health insurance initially; register with ZZZS after residency
- [ ] **HRT**: Get 3-6 month supply and doctor's letter (translated to Slovenian)
- [ ] **Banking**: Consider NLB, Nova KBM, or Revolut
- [ ] **Housing**: Research rental market (nepremicnine.si, bolha.com)
- [ ] **Language**: Slovenian essential for bureaucracy; English in Ljubljana
- [ ] **Document translation**: Official Slovenian translations needed

### After Arrival
- [ ] Register residence with Administrative Unit (Upravna enota)
- [ ] Obtain EMŠO (personal identification number)
- [ ] Register with ZZZS (Health Insurance Institute)
- [ ] Open local bank account
- [ ] Get local SIM card (Telekom Slovenije, A1, Telemach)
- [ ] Connect with LGBTQ+ community (Legebitra, TransAkcija)
- [ ] Register with University Medical Centre Ljubljana for trans healthcare

## Practical Information

### Cost of Living
- **Moderate** — comparable to southern EU
- Monthly rent (1-bed): €500-900 (Ljubljana), €350-600 (Maribor), €300-500 (other cities)
- Monthly groceries: €250-400
- Public transport: €30-50/month (Ljubljana)
- Dining out: €10-20 per meal

### Best Cities for LGBTQ+ Community
| City | LGBTQ+ Scene | Trans Healthcare |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Ljubljana** | Good (Pride, bars, community) | Best (UKCL) |
| **Maribor** | Moderate | Limited |
| **Koper** | Limited | None |

### Climate
- **Continental**: Cold winters (-5°C to 5°C), warm summers (20-30°C)
- **Coastal (Primorska)**: Mediterranean influence, milder winters
- **Alpine**: Colder, snow in winter
- **Best time to arrive**: Spring or early autumn

### Language
- Official language: Slovenian (South Slavic language)
- English: Widely spoken in Ljubljana, especially among under 40s
- Other languages: Croatian, Serbian, and Bosnian widely understood
- Free Slovenian language courses available for immigrants
- Slovenian is essential for official documents and healthcare
