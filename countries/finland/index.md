## Legal Overview

- **Gender Recognition:** Self-ID since 2023. Adults 18+ can self-declare to the Digital and Population Data Services Agency (DVV). No medical requirements, no surgery, no therapy.
- **Anti-Discrimination Law:** Comprehensive, covering gender identity in employment, housing, and services (Non-Discrimination Act 2014).
- **Hate Crime Laws:** Gender identity included in criminal code.
- **Same-Sex Marriage:** Legal since 2017.
- **Conversion Therapy:** Banned (2025).


## Healthcare Reality

- **HRT Access:** Informed consent available. Helsinki's HUS Gender Identity Clinic is the main provider. Wait times: 6–12 months for first appointment.
- **HRT Cost:** €5–15 per prescription (public). €30–50/month private.
- **Regional variation:** Helsinki has good access. Other regions require travel to Helsinki for specialist care.
- **Surgery:** Publicly funded (HUS clinic). Wait times: 1–2 years.


## Where to Live

- **Helsinki:** Largest trans community. Best healthcare access. 1-bedroom: €900–1,400/month.
- **Tampere:** Small but active community. 1-bedroom: €700–1,000/month.
- **Turku:** Small community. Good university town. 1-bedroom: €600–900/month.
- **Rural Finland:** Very limited trans community. High social isolation risk.

**Monthly budget (Helsinki):** €1,500–2,500.

## Known Risks

1. **Long, dark winters** — extreme seasonal affect. Helsinki gets 6 hours of daylight in December.
2. **Language:** Finnish is exceptionally difficult. Swedish is easier but less widely spoken as a first language.
3. **Healthcare wait times** for the Gender Identity Clinic are long.
4. **Small population** means limited dating pool and community.
5. **High alcohol prices** and limited social life outside Helsinki.
