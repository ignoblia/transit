# Moving to Ireland

## Immigration Pathways

### EEA/EU Citizens
Free movement applies. No registration requirement for EU citizens, but you need a valid passport or national ID card. After 5 years of residence, you can apply for permanent residence.

### UK Citizens
Under the Common Travel Area (CTA), UK citizens have the right to live, work, and access services in Ireland without a visa. No registration required, but you should bring proof of residence and access healthcare through the HSE.

### Third-Country Nationals
- **Critical Skills Employment Permit**: For occupations on the Critical Skills List (ICT, engineering, healthcare). Fast-track processing, path to long-term residence after 2 years.
- **General Employment Permit**: For other job offers. Labour market test required. Valid for 2 years.
- **Stamp 4 (Long Stay)**: For researchers, academics, and family members.
- **Student Visa**: For study at recognised institutions. Part-time work permitted (20 hours/week term, 40 hours/week holidays).
- **Family Reunification**: For spouses, civil partners, and de facto partners (2+ years cohabitation).

## Digital Nomad / Remote Work

Ireland does not have a specific digital nomad visa. Remote workers typically enter on a **Stamp 3 (Visitor)** for short stays (up to 90 days, no work allowed for non-EEA), or use the **Critical Skills** or **General Employment Permit** routes if employed by an Irish company. A new digital nomad scheme is being discussed but not yet legislated.

## Moving Checklist

### Before Moving
- [ ] Obtain necessary visa/work permit (non-EU)
- [ ] Arrange health insurance (required for non-EU visa holders)
- [ ] Research housing (Dublin is very expensive; Cork, Galway, Limerick more affordable)
- [ ] Gather medical records and HRT documentation (English is fine)
- [ ] Notify your bank and redirect mail

### After Arrival
- [ ] Register with immigration (GNIB/IRP card) — within 90 days for non-EU
- [ ] Apply for PPS (Personal Public Service) number — essential for work, healthcare, taxes
- [ ] Register with a GP (find trans-friendly GPs through TENI directory)
- [ ] Open an Irish bank account (AIB, Bank of Ireland, Revolut)
- [ ] Apply for a Public Services Card
- [ ] Get an Irish SIM card (Vodafie, Three, Eir)
- [ ] Register with the National Gender Service or find a private endocrinologist
- [ ] Connect with TENI for local trans community resources

## Practical Information

### Cost of Living
- Monthly rent (1-bed): €1,500-2,200 (Dublin), €900-1,400 (Cork, Galway)
- Monthly budget (single person): €1,000-1,500 excluding rent
- Public transport: €80-120/month (Dublin Leap Card), limited in rural areas

### Climate
Temperate maritime — mild winters (4-8°C), cool summers (15-20°C). Frequent rain year-round. Rarely snows.

### Language
English and Irish (Gaeilge) are official languages. English is the primary language of daily life. Irish is spoken in Gaeltacht regions but is not needed for living in Ireland.
