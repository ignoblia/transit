# Healthcare & HRT in Latvia

## Gatekeeping Model
Latvia has **very limited** transgender healthcare services:

- **Psychiatric diagnosis required** — via a state-appointed medical commission
- **Mandatory real-life experience** period before HRT
- **Surgery required** for legal gender recognition (see Legal Rights)
- Services concentrated in **Riga**; virtually none outside the capital

## Accessing HRT

### Step 1: Initial Consultation
- Contact the **Riga East University Hospital** (RAKUS) Psychiatry Clinic
- Very limited number of specialists available
- Long waiting times (3-8 months for initial appointment)
- Latvian language required for most appointments

### Step 2: Medical Commission
- A multi-disciplinary commission evaluates your case
- Must include psychiatrists, endocrinologists, and other specialists
- Diagnosis of gender dysphoria required
- Commission approval needed for treatment to proceed

### Step 3: Hormone Therapy
- HRT prescribed by an endocrinologist after commission approval
- Limited choice of medications (only standard generics)
- Monitoring every 3-6 months
- Public healthcare covers basic HRT medications

## Key Clinics

| Clinic | Location | Services |
|--------|----------|----------|
| Riga East University Hospital (RAKUS) | Riga | Psychiatric assessment, medical commission |
| Pauls Stradins Clinical University Hospital | Riga | Endocrinology, HRT |
| Private psychiatrists | Riga | Assessment (shorter wait times, €60-100) |
| Private endocrinologists | Riga | HRT (limited availability) |

## Prescription Portability
- **EU prescriptions**: Valid in Latvia under EU mutual recognition
- **Non-EU prescriptions**: Must be verified by a Latvian doctor
- **Importing medication**: 3-month supply permitted with a doctor's letter
- **Limited availability**: Some HRT medications may need to be ordered from other EU countries
- **Pharmacies**: Large chain pharmacies (Mēness, Euroaptieka) in cities may stock HRT

## Surgical Options
- **No gender-affirming surgeries available** in Latvia
- All surgeries must be arranged abroad (Thailand, Germany, Serbia, Belgium)
- Public funding for surgery abroad is extremely limited
- Most pay out-of-pocket for surgery abroad
- Top surgery abroad: €5,000-8,000
- Bottom surgery abroad: €15,000-25,000
