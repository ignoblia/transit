# Moving to the United Kingdom

## Immigration Pathways

The UK operates a **points-based immigration system** after leaving the EU. EU citizens no longer have free movement rights and must apply under the same system as other nationalities.

### Skilled Worker Visa (Most Common)

| Requirement | Details |
|------------|---------|
| Job offer | From a Home Office-approved sponsor |
| Skill level | RQF Level 3 or above (A-level equivalent) |
| Salary threshold | £26,200/year (or £10.75/hour, whichever higher) |
| English language | B1 level required |
| Points needed | 70 points total |
| Duration | Up to 5 years |
| Settlement | Eligible after 5 years (Indefinite Leave to Remain) |

**Points breakdown:**
- Job at appropriate skill level: 20 points
- English language: 10 points
- Salary £26,200+ or relevant PhD: 20 points
- Salary above £23,580 (minimum): additional points from tradeable options

### Global Talent Visa

- For leaders or potential leaders in academia, arts, culture, digital technology
- No job offer required
- Endorsement from recognized UK body (e.g., Royal Society, Arts Council England)
- Valid for 3-5 years
- Fast track to settlement (3 years)
- Can be used by trans activists, researchers, academics

### Other Visa Options

| Visa Type | Description | Requirements | Duration |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|----------|
| Student Visa | Full-time study | University acceptance (CAS), funds proof | Duration of studies |
| Graduate Visa | After completing UK degree | Valid for 2 years (3 for PhD) | 2-3 years |
| Health and Care Worker Visa | Healthcare professionals | Job offer in health or social care | Up to 5 years |
| Family Visa | Partner/family of UK resident | Relationship proof, financial requirement (£29,000+) | 2.5 years, renewable |
| Innovator Founder Visa | Business creation | Endorsed business plan, funds | 3 years |
| Youth Mobility Scheme | 18-30 years (select countries) | Working holiday, lottery-based | 2 years |

### EU Citizens (Pre-Settled / Settled Status)

- EU citizens who lived in the UK before 31 December 2020 should have applied to the **EU Settlement Scheme**
- **Settled status**: 5+ years continuous residence before 2021
- **Pre-settled status**: Less than 5 years
- After 2021: EU citizens need a visa like everyone else

## Digital Nomad / Remote Work

The UK does **not** have a specific digital nomad visa. Remote workers can:

- Enter as a **Standard Visitor** (up to 6 months) — but cannot work for a UK employer
- Apply for a **Skilled Worker Visa** if employed by a UK company
- Use a **Global Talent Visa** if eligible
- Some use the **Youth Mobility Scheme** (age and nationality dependent)
- Remote work for a non-UK company while on a visitor visa is legally unclear

## Moving Checklist

### Before Moving
- [ ] **Passport**: Valid for at least 6 months
- [ ] **Visa**: Apply well in advance (Skilled Worker: 3-8 weeks processing)
- [ ] **Health Surcharge**: Pay Immigration Health Surcharge (£1,035/year per person)
- [ ] **HRT**: Get 3-6 month supply and doctor's letter
- [ ] **Medical Records**: Relevant records
- [ ] **Banking**: Consider opening a UK bank account from abroad
- [ ] **Housing**: Research rental market (Rightmove, Zoopla)
- [ ] **Language**: English — no issues for most

### After Arrival
- [ ] **Collect Biometric Residence Permit (BRP)** — within 10 days
- [ ] **Register with GP** — Register with a local GP surgery (NHS)
- [ ] **National Insurance Number** — Apply for NI number for work
- [ ] **Open bank account** — Monzo, Starling, or high street bank
- [ ] **Get UK phone number** — Giffgaff, EE, Vodafone, O2
- [ ] **Register with NHS Gender Identity Clinic** — Put yourself on the waitlist immediately
- [ ] **Connect with local trans community** — Stonewall, Local LGBTQ+ groups

### Trans-Specific Considerations

- **NHS waiting times are very long** (2-5 years) — join the GIC waitlist immediately
- **Private healthcare** (GenderGP, Gendercare) is a viable option while waiting
- **Scotland** generally has better trans protections and shorter wait times
- **Transphobia** is a growing concern, particularly online and in some media
- **London, Brighton, Manchester, Glasgow** are the most trans-friendly cities
- **Northern Ireland** has the most restrictive environment
- **Trans rights** are highly politicized — monitor the political situation
- **Equality Act 2010** provides strong legal protections in theory

### Language
- **English**: Spoken at all levels
- **Regional languages**: Welsh (Wales), Scots Gaelic (Scotland), Irish (NI)
- No language test for most visas (except citizenship — B1 English + Life in the UK test)

### Citizenship Timeline
- 5 years on most visas → Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR)
- 12 months after ILR → Citizenship application
- Life in the UK test required
- B1 English language requirement
