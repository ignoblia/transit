# Legal Rights in Slovenia

## Gender Recognition
Slovenia operates a **medicalized model** for legal gender recognition, though reforms are under discussion:

- **Requirements**: Diagnosis of gender dysphoria from a licensed psychiatrist + evidence of HRT
- **Surgery**: Not explicitly required for legal recognition, though medical evidence of treatment (HRT) is needed
- **Age**: 18+ can apply independently; 15-18 with parental consent
- **Process**: Application to the Administrative Unit (Upravna enota) with medical documentation
- **Timeframe**: Typically 2-4 months

### Current Process
1. Referral from a GP to a psychiatrist or endocrinologist specializing in gender dysphoria
2. Diagnosis and treatment plan (HRT)
3. Application to the Administrative Unit with medical evidence
4. Updated birth certificate, ID card, and passport
5. Notification to relevant institutions

**Reform Note**: Civil society organizations (Legebitra, TransAkcija) are advocating for a self-determination model. No legislation has been passed as of 2026.

## Name Changes
- Name change is processed through the **Administrative Unit**
- Must align with the gender marker change
- Can be done simultaneously with gender recognition
- Process typically takes 1-2 months

## Anti-Discrimination Laws
Slovenia has strong anti-discrimination protections:

- **Employment**: Protected under the Employment Relationships Act
- **Education**: Protection in schools and universities
- **Healthcare**: Access to trans healthcare is legally recognized
- **Housing**: General anti-discrimination provisions apply
- **Goods and Services**: Protected under the Protection Against Discrimination Act

The **Protection Against Discrimination Act (ZVarD)** explicitly includes gender identity as a protected characteristic. The **Equal Opportunities Ombudsperson** handles complaints.

## Hate Crime Laws
- The **Criminal Code** explicitly includes gender identity as a protected characteristic in hate crime provisions (Article 131)
- Enhanced penalties for crimes motivated by transphobia
- Police have specialized LGBTQ+ liaison officers
- Prosecution is improving, with several successful cases in recent years

## Document Recognition
- Updated passports and ID cards are recognized internationally
- Slovenia has biometric ID cards and passports with updated gender markers
- Previous documents are sealed and not publicly accessible
- EU mutual recognition applies for travel within the Schengen Area
