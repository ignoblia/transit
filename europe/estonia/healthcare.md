# Healthcare & HRT in Estonia

## Gatekeeping Model
Estonia operates a **gatekeeping model** for transgender healthcare:

- **Psychiatric diagnosis required** to access HRT
- Must be referred by a psychiatrist to an endocrinologist
- Treatment decisions made by medical professionals, not solely by patient
- Real-life experience period may be required before HRT

## Accessing HRT

### Step 1: Psychiatric Assessment
- Contact a psychiatrist specializing in gender dysphoria
- Available in Tallinn (North Estonia Medical Centre) and Tartu (Tartu University Hospital)
- Waiting times: 2-6 months for initial appointment
- Multiple sessions typically required for diagnosis

### Step 2: Diagnosis
- F64.0 (Transsexualism) or similar ICD diagnosis
- Psychological evaluation and medical history review
- Some clinics require 3-6 months of regular appointments

### Step 3: Endocrinology Referral
- Referred to an endocrinologist for HRT
- Blood tests for baseline hormone levels
- Treatment plan developed by the medical team
- Follow-up every 3-6 months initially

## Key Clinics

| Clinic | Location | Services |
|--------|----------|----------|
| North Estonia Medical Centre (PERH) | Tallinn | Psychiatric assessment, endocrinology |
| Tartu University Hospital | Tartu | Full gender clinic services |
| Private psychiatrists | Tallinn, Tartu | Assessment (shorter wait times) |
| Private endocrinologists | Tallinn | HRT prescription and monitoring |

## Prescription Portability
- **EU/EEA prescriptions**: Valid in Estonia under EU mutual recognition
- **Non-EU prescriptions**: Must be verified by an Estonian doctor
- **Importing medication**: 3-month supply permitted with a doctor's letter
- **Keep in original packaging** with prescription labels
- **Know generic names** of your medications for local equivalents

## Surgical Options
- Gender-affirming surgeries are **not routinely available** in Estonia
- Some basic procedures (top surgery) available at Tallinn private clinics
- Most Estonians travel abroad for surgeries (Thailand, Germany, Belgium)
- Public funding for surgery abroad may be available in exceptional cases
- Prior authorization from the Estonian Health Insurance Fund (Haigekassa) required

## Costs

| Service | Estonian Resident | Non-Resident |
|---------|------------------|--------------|
| Public healthcare | Free (covered by health insurance) | Not available |
| Psychiatric assessment | Free (public) | €100-200 |
| Endocrinology visit | Free (public) | €80-150 |
| HRT (monthly) | €10-30 | €30-60 |
| Private psychiatrist | €60-100 | €60-100 |
