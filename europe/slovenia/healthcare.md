# Healthcare & HRT in Slovenia

## Gatekeeping Model
Slovenia operates a **gatekeeping model** for transgender healthcare:

- **Psychiatric diagnosis required** to access HRT
- Treatment centralized at the **University Medical Centre Ljubljana** (UKCL)
- Multidisciplinary team involved in treatment decisions
- Public healthcare covers most costs

## Accessing HRT

### Step 1: Referral
- Contact a GP or directly reach the **University Medical Centre Ljubljana**, Department of Psychiatry
- Referral from a GP is the standard pathway
- Initial appointment wait time: 2-4 months

### Step 2: Psychiatric Assessment
- 3-5 sessions with a psychiatrist specializing in gender dysphoria
- Diagnosis per ICD-10/11 criteria
- Psychological evaluation and treatment planning

### Step 3: Hormone Therapy
- Referral to an endocrinologist at UKCL
- Baseline blood tests for hormone levels
- HRT prescribed under medical supervision
- Follow-up every 3-6 months initially
- Annual monitoring thereafter

## Key Clinics

| Clinic | Location | Services |
|--------|----------|----------|
| University Medical Centre Ljubljana | Ljubljana | Full gender clinic (public) |
| Maribor University Medical Centre | Maribor | Limited gender services |
| Private psychiatrists | Ljubljana | Assessment (shorter wait times, €70-120) |
| Private endocrinologists | Ljubljana, Maribor | HRT (limited trans experience) |

## Prescription Portability
- **EU prescriptions**: Valid in Slovenia under EU mutual recognition
- **Non-EU prescriptions**: Must be evaluated by a Slovenian doctor
- **Importing medication**: 3-month supply permitted with a doctor's letter
- **Keep in original packaging** with prescription labels
- **Pharmacies**: Lekarna (national chain) stocks standard HRT medications
- **Generic names**: Know your medications (estradiol, cyproterone, testosterone)

## Surgical Options
- Gender-affirming surgeries are **covered by public healthcare** (ZZZS)
- Authorization requires diagnosis + documented HRT period (typically 1-2 years)
- Surgery is performed abroad through public funding agreements (usually in Serbia or Germany)
- Alternative: self-funded surgery abroad
- Top surgery: covered through public system or private (€4,000-7,000)
- Bottom surgery: available through public funding abroad
- Facial feminization: generally not covered by public healthcare
