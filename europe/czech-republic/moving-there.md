# Moving to the Czech Republic

## Immigration Pathways

### EU/EEA/Swiss Citizens

The Czech Republic grants full **free movement rights** to EU/EEA/Swiss citizens:

- **Up to 30 days**: No registration required
- **Over 30 days**: Must register with the Foreign Police (Cizinecká policie)
- **Over 3 months**: Apply for **pobytové oprávnění** (residence permit) at the Ministry of Interior
- **Requirements**: Valid passport or ID, proof of employment, sufficient funds, or health insurance
- **Right to work**: Automatic
- **After 5 years**: Eligible for permanent residence
- **After 5 years permanent residence**: Eligible for citizenship (language test required)

### Non-EU Citizens

| Visa Type | Description | Requirements | Duration |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|----------|
| Employee Card | Employment with Czech employer | Job offer (no labour market test needed for most jobs), degree | Up to 2 years, renewable |
| EU Blue Card | Highly qualified workers | Degree + salary threshold (approx. €45,000+/year) | 2 years, renewable |
| Student Visa | Full-time study | University acceptance, funds proof (approx. €5,000+/year) | Duration of studies |
| Family Reunification | Family of resident | Relationship proof, accommodation, income | 1 year, renewable |
| Zivno (Freelance Visa) | Self-employment | Trade license (Živnostenský list), business plan, client list | 1 year, renewable |

### Zivno (Freelance) Visa

The **Zivno visa** is a popular option for remote workers and freelancers:

- Requires obtaining a **Živnostenský list** (trade license)
- Must show a client base (at least partial in Czech Republic)
- Business plan demonstrating viability
- Minimum income requirements (varies)
- Valid for 1 year, renewable
- Relatively fast processing compared to other visas
- Taxes: ~15% income tax + social/health insurance
- Accountant highly recommended (Czech tax system is complex)

## Digital Nomad / Remote Work

The Czech Republic does **not** have a specific digital nomad visa. Remote work options:

- **Zivno (Freelance) visa** is the most popular remote work route
- **Employee Card** if employed by a Czech company
- **Long-term visa for self-employment** — another option for independent workers
- Remote workers on employee cards must have a Czech employer
- Zivno is flexible for freelancers with international clients

## Moving Checklist

### Before Moving
- [ ] **Passport**: Valid for at least 6 months
- [ ] **Visa/Residence**: Check requirements for your nationality
- [ ] **Health Insurance**: EU citizens: EHIC; others: private insurance (then public after registration)
- [ ] **HRT**: Get 3-6 month supply and doctor's letter (English or Czech)
- [ ] **Medical Records**: Translated into Czech
- [ ] **Banking**: International banking options
- [ ] **Housing**: Research rental market (sreality.cz, bezrealitky.cz)
- [ ] **Language**: Czech is very helpful but English is sufficient in Prague

### After Arrival
- [ ] **Register with Foreign Police** within 30 days
- [ ] **Apply for residence permit** (pobytové oprávnění) — EU citizens
- [ ] **Get Czech phone number** — T-Mobile, Vodafone, O2
- [ ] **Register with VZP** (public health insurance)
- [ ] **Open bank account** — Česká spořitelna, ČSOB, Moneta, Air Bank
- [ ] **Get Czech ID card** if staying long-term
- [ ] **Register with sexology clinic** if needing HRT
- [ ] **Connect with Trans*parent** or Prague Pride community

### Language
- **Official language**: Czech
- **English**: Widely spoken in Prague, especially among under 40s
- **German**: Also somewhat common
- **Brno**: Good English spoken (large student population)
- **Smaller cities**: English much less common
- **Integration**: Czech is essential for life outside Prague
- Free Czech courses available for immigrants in some municipalities

### Climate
- **Continental climate**: Cold winters, warm summers
- **Winter**: -10°C to 5°C (December-February)
- **Summer**: 15°C to 30°C (June-August)
- **Best time to arrive**: Spring (April-May) or early autumn (September)

### Trans-Specific Considerations
- **Prague is the most trans-friendly** city — large LGBTQ+ scene
- **Brno** has a growing queer community, especially among students
- Rural areas and smaller towns are very conservative
- Far-right influence is growing but mostly concentrated outside cities
- **Sexuologický ústav** in Prague centralizes trans care — gatekeeping model
- Sterilization requirement for legal gender recognition remains a major issue
- **Trans*parent** provides community support and advocacy
- Czech society is generally secular but socially conservative on gender issues
- Growing expat community, especially in Prague and Brno
