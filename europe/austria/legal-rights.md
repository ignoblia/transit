# Legal Rights in Austria

## Gender Recognition

Austria adopted a **self-determination model** for legal gender recognition in 2024, replacing the previous medicalized system. This was a landmark reform following years of advocacy.

### Current Process (since 2024)

- **Self-declaration** to the civil registry (Standesamt)
- No medical certificates, diagnosis, surgery, or therapy required
- No waiting period
- Minimum age: 14 (with parental consent under 18)
- Process is administrative — typically completed within a few weeks

### Previous System (before 2024)

Previously, Austria required:
- Psychiatric diagnosis of gender dysphoria
- Court application and hearing
- Medical reports from multiple specialists
- This was widely criticized as invasive and pathologizing

## Name Changes

- Name changes are submitted alongside the gender recognition application
- Or can be done independently through the civil registry
- No medical requirements
- Simple administrative process
- Usually processed within 2-4 weeks

## Anti-Discrimination Laws

Austria has comprehensive anti-discrimination protections:

- **Equal Treatment Act (Gleichbehandlungsgesetz)** — Prohibits discrimination based on gender
- **Gender identity** is recognized as falling under gender discrimination protections
- **Employment** — Full protection in all employment aspects (hiring, promotion, dismissal)
- **Education** — Protection in schools and universities
- **Goods and Services** — Access to services without discrimination
- **Housing** — Protection in rental and purchase
- **Social Security** — Equal access to benefits

The **Equal Treatment Commission** (Gleichbehandlungskommission) handles discrimination complaints.

## Hate Crime Laws

- Austria's Criminal Code includes hate crime provisions with enhanced penalties
- Gender identity is recognized as a protected characteristic
- Covers violent crimes, threats, harassment, and hate speech
- Reporting and prosecution rates are generally good
- Vienna has specialized police units for hate crime

## Document Recognition

- Updated gender markers on Austrian passports and IDs are recognized internationally
- Austrian passports can use updated marker without additional requirements
- Third gender / "divers" marker available since 2018
- Birth certificate updates follow the self-declaration process
- Documents accepted across the EU
