# Moving to Austria

## Immigration Pathways

### EU/EEA/Swiss Citizens

Austria grants full **free movement rights** to EU/EEA/Swiss citizens:

- **Up to 3 months**: No registration required
- **Over 3 months**: Must register with the municipal office (Meldeamt) and obtain an **Anmeldebescheinigung** (registration certificate)
- **Right to work**: Automatic
- **After 5 years**: Eligible for permanent residence (Daueraufenthalt)
- **After 6-10 years**: Eligible for Austrian citizenship (requires German language B2, citizenship test)

### Non-EU Citizens

The **Red-White-Red Card** system is the main immigration pathway:

| Card Type | Description | Requirements | Duration |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|----------|
| Red-White-Red Card | Work permit | Job offer, minimum salary (€3,000+/month gross), degree | 2 years, renewable |
| Red-White-Red Card (Key Worker) | Highly skilled workers | Degree in high-demand field, salary threshold | 2 years, renewable |
| Red-White-Red Card (Start-up) | Founders | Innovative business plan, 50,000+ EUR funds | 2 years, renewable |
| Red-White-Red Card Plus | Free job mobility | After 5 years or for family members | Unlimited |
| Student Visa | Full-time study | University acceptance, funds proof (€6,000+/year) | 1 year, renewable |
| Family Reunification | Family of residents | Relationship proof, accommodation, health insurance | 1 year, renewable |
| EU Blue Card | Highly qualified | Degree + salary ≥ €60,000+/year | 2 years, renewable |

### Digital Nomad / Remote Work

Austria does **not** have a specific digital nomad visa. Remote workers can:

- Apply for a **Red-White-Red Card** if employed by an Austrian company
- **Freelancer visa**: Available for self-employed individuals with Austrian clients
- **Residence permit for exceptional circumstances**: Limited options for remote workers
- Many remote workers use EU citizenship or the start-up visa route

## Moving Checklist

### Before Moving
- [ ] **Passport**: Valid for at least 6 months
- [ ] **Visa/Residence**: Check requirements for your nationality
- [ ] **Health Insurance**: Mandatory — register with Austrian social insurance (ÖGK)
- [ ] **HRT**: Get 3-6 month supply and doctor's letter (German preferred)
- [ ] **Medical Records**: Translated into German
- [ ] **Banking**: International banking options
- [ ] **Housing**: Research rental market (willhaben.at, immobilienscout24.at)
- [ ] **Language**: German is essential for integration

### After Arrival
- [ ] **Register address** at the Meldeamt (within 3 days!)
- [ ] **Apply for residence permit** (EU citizens: get Anmeldebescheinigung)
- [ ] **Get social insurance** through employer or ÖGK
- [ ] **Open bank account** — Erste Bank, Raiffeisen, Bank Austria
- [ ] **Get e-card** (health insurance card)
- [ ] **Register with AKH Genderambulanz** if in Vienna
- [ ] **Connect with HOSI Wien** for LGBTQ+ community

## Practical Information

### Cost of Living
| Category | Vienna | Other Cities |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| Rent (1-bedroom) | €800-1,300/month | €500-900/month |
| Groceries | €250-400/month | €220-350/month |
| Public transport | €33/month (annual pass) | €30-50/month |
| Health insurance | Social insurance included | Social insurance included |
| Utilities | €150-250/month | €120-200/month |

### Language
- **Official language**: German
- **English**: Widely spoken in Vienna, especially in business and tech
- **Integration**: German B1 recommended for daily life, B2 required for citizenship
- Free German courses available through integration programs
- Viennese dialect is distinct from standard German

### Climate
- **Continental climate**: Cold winters, warm summers
- **Winter**: -5°C to 5°C (December-February)
- **Summer**: 15°C to 30°C (June-August)
- **Best time to arrive**: Spring (March-May) or early autumn

### Trans-Specific Considerations
- **Vienna is the most trans-friendly city** — AKH Genderambulanz provides excellent care
- Graz, Linz, and Salzburg are generally accepting
- Rural and conservative areas (Styria, Carinthia, Tyrol) may be less welcoming
- Self-ID law (2024) is a major positive
- **HOSI Wien** offers community support
- Trans* Austria provides advocacy and resources
