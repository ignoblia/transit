# Moving to Sweden

## Immigration Pathways

### EU/EEA/Swiss Citizens
Sweden grants full free movement rights to EU/EEA/Swiss citizens. Registration with the Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket) is required for stays over one year.

### Non-EU Citizens

| Visa Type | Description | Requirements | Duration |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|----------|
| Work Visa | For employment with Swedish employer | Job offer, salary ≥ ~€26,000/year, union-approved terms | 2 years, renewable |
| EU Blue Card | For highly qualified workers | Degree + salary threshold (approx. €55,000/year) | 2 years, renewable |
| Self-Employed Visa | For business owners/freelancers | Business plan, sufficient funds, client base in Sweden | 2 years, renewable |
| Family Reunification | For family of Swedish residents | Relationship proof, sufficient income (€8,800+/year after housing) | 2 years, renewable |
| Student Visa | For full-time study | University acceptance, funds proof (€9,000+/year) | Duration of studies |
| Research Visa | For researchers | Host institution agreement | 2 years, renewable |

## Key Relocation Steps

1. **Secure a job or study place** — Job offer is the most common path for non-EU citizens
2. **Apply for residence permit** — Through the Swedish Migration Agency (before arrival for non-EU)
3. **Register with Skatteverket** — Get a personal identity number (personnummer)
4. **Find accommodation** — Housing is competitive, especially in Stockholm and Gothenburg
5. **Open a bank account** — Requires personnummer (BankID comes later)
6. **Register with a healthcare centre (vårdcentral)** — For a GP
7. **Enrol in Swedish language courses (SFI)** — Free for residents
8. **Join RFSL** — For LGBTQ+ community support

## Cost of Living

Sweden has a high cost of living, particularly in major cities:
- Rent (1-bedroom): €900-1,500/month (Stockholm); €600-1,000 (Gothenburg/Malmö)
- Groceries: €300-450/month
- Public transport: €80-120/month pass
- Health insurance: Public system, low costs (annual cap ~€240 for medications)
- Second-hand market (Blocket, Myrorna) is excellent for affordable furniture

## Trans-Specific Relocation Tips

- **Healthcare wait times are long** — Register with a gender clinic immediately upon arrival
- **Stockholm's ANOVA clinic** has the longest wait times; consider Gothenburg or Umeå for shorter waits
- **English is very widely spoken** — nearly all Swedes speak fluent English
- **Swedish is essential for integration** — and required for permanent residence and citizenship
- **Bring medical records translated into Swedish**
- **Carry at least 6 months of HRT medication** given the long wait times for gender clinics
- **Private HRT options** are limited — Gender GP (UK-based) is used by some, but prescriptions may face issues
- **High Swedish requirement for citizenship** — C1 level language test required
- **Sweden has a strong social safety net** — unemployment benefits, parental leave, healthcare subsidies

## Language

- **Official language**: Swedish
- **English** — Very widely spoken, over 90% fluency
- **Swedish** required for permanent residence (A2-B1) and citizenship (C1 — high requirement)
- **SFI (Swedish for Immigrants)** — Free language courses provided by municipalities
- Swedish is one of the hardest requirements for naturalisation in the EU

## Citizenship Timeline

- 5 years of continuous residence (EU/EEA citizens) or 5 years (non-EU citizens)
- Must demonstrate Swedish language proficiency at C1 level
- Good conduct requirement (no serious criminal record)
- Citizenship interview may be required
