# Healthcare & HRT in Hungary

## Gatekeeping Model
Hungary's transgender healthcare system is **restricted and under increasing political pressure**:

- **Psychiatric diagnosis required** for access to HRT
- Services concentrated in **Budapest**
- Growing difficulty accessing care due to political climate
- Some providers have stopped offering trans healthcare due to fear of repercussions

## Accessing HRT

### Step 1: Psychiatric Assessment
- Contact a psychiatrist or sexologist in Budapest
- Major hospitals include Semmelweis University and the National Institute of Psychiatry
- Diagnosis of gender dysphoria per ICD-10/11 required
- Multiple sessions over several months
- Wait times are increasing as providers leave the field

### Step 2: Endocrinology Referral
- Referral to an endocrinologist for hormone therapy
- Baseline blood tests required
- HRT prescribed and monitored
- Follow-up visits every 3-6 months

### Step 3: Ongoing Care
- Regular monitoring by endocrinologist
- Some medications may need to be sourced from abroad
- Growing difficulty finding providers willing to treat trans patients

## Key Clinics

| Clinic | Location | Services |
|--------|----------|----------|
| Semmelweis University | Budapest | Psychiatry, endocrinology |
| National Institute of Psychiatry | Budapest | Psychiatric assessment |
| Private sexologists | Budapest | Assessment (shorter waits) |
| Private endocrinologists | Budapest | HRT (limited trans experience) |

## Prescription Portability
- **EU prescriptions**: Valid in Hungary under EU mutual recognition
- **Non-EU prescriptions**: Must be re-evaluated by a Hungarian doctor
- **Importing medication**: 3-month supply permitted with a doctor's letter
- **Stock issues**: Some HRT medications may be unavailable
- **Pharmacies**: Major chains (Gyógyszertár) in Budapest and larger cities

## Surgical Options
- **No gender-affirming surgeries available** in Hungary
- Previous providers have discontinued services
- All surgeries require travel abroad
- No public funding available
- No established referral pathways
