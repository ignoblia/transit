# Healthcare in Sweden

## Gender Affirming Healthcare

Sweden operates a gatekeeping model for gender affirming healthcare, centralised through **ANOVA** at Karolinska University Hospital in Stockholm, with regional clinics across the country. This is the most challenging aspect of trans life in Sweden — wait times remain long despite the 2025 legal reforms.

## HRT Access

Hormone therapy in Sweden requires:
- Referral to a gender clinic (ANOVA or regional clinic)
- Diagnostic assessment for gender dysphoria (typically 3-12 months of appointments)
- Diagnosis of gender dysphoria usually required
- Endocrinology consult and hormone initiation
- Regular follow-up monitoring

Wait times:
- Stockholm (ANOVA): 12-24 months for first appointment
- Gothenburg (Ågrenska): 12-24 months
- Lund/Malmö: 12-24 months
- Umeå: 6-12 months (shorter wait, smaller clinic)
- Linköping: 12-18 months

## Private/Alternative Options

Private healthcare options are limited in Sweden:
- **Private endocrinologists** — Few offer trans care independently; costs €100-200/session
- **Gender GP (UK-based)** — Some Swedish patients use this for prescriptions, but Swedish pharmacies may not accept foreign prescriptions easily
- **DIY/import** — Some patients import medications, but customs can be strict

## Surgical Options

Gender affirming surgeries are covered by the public healthcare system (Regionen), but wait times are significant:
- **Chest/breast surgery (top surgery)** — Covered, wait times 1-2 years
- **Genital surgery (bottom surgery)** — Available, wait times 1-3 years
- **Facial feminisation surgery** — Very limited public coverage, typically self-funded (€10,000-40,000)
- **Hysterectomy/oophorectomy** — Covered
- **Voice surgery** — Limited availability

## Prescription Portability

As an EU member state, Sweden accepts prescriptions from other EU/EEA countries:
- Valid for up to 30 days
- English or Swedish prescriptions recommended
- Swedish doctors may be reluctant to continue prescriptions from non-EU sources
- For ongoing treatment, a Swedish GP or endocrinologist must re-prescribe

| Medication | Typical Cost (high-cost protection) | Pharmacy Availability |
|------------|-----------------------------------|----------------------|
| Oestradiol (oral/gel/patches) | ~€5-10/month (after deductible cap) | Widely available |
| Testosterone (gel/injectable) | ~€10-20/month (after deductible cap) | Widely available |
| Cyproterone acetate | ~€8-15/month (after deductible cap) | Available |
| GnRH agonists | Covered (specialist only) | Specialist pharmacy |

Sweden's high-cost protection scheme (högkostnadsskydd) caps prescription medication at ~€240/year.

## Mental Health Support

- **ANOVA psych team** — Part of the assessment process
- **RFSL** — Peer support groups for trans people
- **Transformering.se** — Online resources and referral database
- **Private gender therapists** — Limited availability, €80-150/session
