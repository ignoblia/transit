# Healthcare & HRT in Lithuania

## Gatekeeping Model
Lithuania has **very limited** transgender healthcare services:

- **Psychiatric diagnosis required** at the only recognized gender clinic
- **Real-life experience and HRT required** before surgery can be considered
- Services concentrated in **Vilnius** at Santaros Clinics
- Nearly no options outside the capital

## Accessing HRT

### Step 1: Referral
- Contact **Santaros Clinics** (Vilniaus Santaros Klinikos) Psychiatry Department
- GP referral recommended but direct self-referral sometimes accepted
- Very limited appointment availability (4-8 month wait times)

### Step 2: Psychiatric Assessment
- Diagnosis of gender dysphoria per ICD-10/11
- Multiple sessions (typically 3-6) over several months
- Must demonstrate consistent gender identity and treatment commitment

### Step 3: Hormone Therapy
- Referral to an endocrinologist at Santaros Clinics
- Baseline blood tests required
- HRT prescribed and monitored by the clinic
- Follow-up every 3-6 months
- Public healthcare covers basic HRT medications

## Key Clinics

| Clinic | Location | Services |
|--------|----------|----------|
| Santaros Clinics (Vilnius University Hospital) | Vilnius | Only recognized gender clinic |
| Lithuanian University of Health Sciences (LSMU) | Kaunas | Limited endocrinology |
| Private psychiatrists | Vilnius, Kaunas | Assessment (shorter wait times) |
| Private endocrinologists | Vilnius | HRT (limited trans experience) |

## Prescription Portability
- **EU prescriptions**: Valid in Lithuania under EU mutual recognition
- **Non-EU prescriptions**: Must be re-evaluated by a Lithuanian doctor
- **Importing medication**: 3-month supply permitted with a doctor's letter
- **Limited stock**: Some HRT medications may need to be ordered from abroad
- **Pharmacies**: Major chains (Euro vaistinė, Gintarinė vaistinė) in cities
- **Generic names**: Know your medications' generic names for local equivalents

## Surgical Options
- **No gender-affirming surgeries available** in Lithuania
- Previously performed at Santaros Clinics but discontinued
- All surgeries require travel abroad (Thailand, Germany, Serbia, Belgium)
- Theoretical possibility of public funding for abroad surgery is rarely approved
- Most pay out-of-pocket
- Top surgery abroad: €4,000-8,000
- Bottom surgery abroad: €15,000-25,000

## Costs

| Service | Lithuanian Resident | Non-Resident |
|---------|-------------------|--------------|
| Psychiatric assessment | Free (public) | €80-120 |
| Endocrinology visit | Free (public) | €60-100 |
| HRT (monthly) | €10-30 | €20-50 |
| Santaros consultation | Free (public) | €100-150 |
| Surgery abroad | Not covered | N/A |
