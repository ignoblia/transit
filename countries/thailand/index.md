## Legal Overview

- **Gender Recognition:** Medicalised. Surgery required for legal gender marker change. No anti-discrimination law at federal level. Marriage Equality Act (2024) legalised same-sex marriage.
- **Anti-Discrimination Law:** None specific to gender identity or sexual orientation at federal level.
- **Hate Crime Laws:** None.
- **Same-Sex Marriage:** Legal since 2024 (Marriage Equality Act).


## Healthcare

- **HRT Access:** Informed consent widely available at private clinics. Many clinics are trans-friendly. Low cost by international standards.
- **Cost:** THB 500–2,000/month (≈ €14–56).


## Visa Pathways

| Type | Income Req. | Processing | Leads to PR? |

| Smart Visa | THB 200,000+/month (≈ €5,600) | 1–3 months | Yes |
| Education Visa | Enrolment | 1–2 months | Limited |
| Retirement Visa | THB 800,000 bank deposit | 1–2 months | Renewable |


## Cost of Living (Bangkok)

1-bedroom: THB 10,000–25,000/month (≈ €280–700). Monthly budget: THB 30,000–60,000 (≈ €840–1,680).


## Key Considerations

- **Large kathoey (trans) community** with cultural visibility
- **No legal protections** against discrimination — can be fired, denied housing, etc.
- **Surgery requirement** for legal recognition
- **Affordable HRT and surgeries** — Thailand is a global centre for gender-affirming surgery
- **Safety:** Generally safe for LGBTQ+ people. Violence is less common than in the West but low-level discrimination is frequent
- **Asylum:** Not available for LGBTQ+ claims
- **Visa pathways** for long-term settlement are limited except for retirees or high-income professionals
