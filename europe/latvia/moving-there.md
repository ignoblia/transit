# Moving to Latvia

## Immigration Pathways

### EEA/EU Citizens
- **Free movement rights** — no visa required
- **Up to 3 months**: No registration needed
- **Over 3 months**: Register with the Office of Citizenship and Migration Affairs (PMLP)
- **Right to work**: Automatic
- **Population Register**: Must register your place of residence

### UK Citizens (Post-Brexit)
- **Schengen Area**: Visa-free for up to 90 days in any 180-day period
- **Long-term residency**: Requires a residence permit:
  - Work permit (job offer from Latvian employer)
  - Student visa (enrollment at Latvian university)
  - Family reunification
  - Real estate visa (€250,000+ property investment)

### Third-Country Nationals
| Visa Type | Requirements | Duration |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| Work Permit | Job offer from Latvian employer | Up to 2 years, renewable |
| Student Visa | Enrollment at accredited institution | Duration of studies |
| Family Reunification | Family ties to Latvian resident | Tied to sponsor's status |
| Real Estate Visa | €250,000+ property investment | 5 years, renewable |
| Start-up Visa | Innovative business idea | Up to 2 years |

## Digital Nomad / Remote Work
Latvia does **not have a dedicated digital nomad visa**. Remote workers can consider:
- **Work visa** if employed by a Latvian company
- **Start-up visa** for entrepreneurs
- **Temporary residence permit** through real estate investment
- Some remote workers enter on a 90-day tourist visa for short stays, but this does not permit work

## Moving Checklist

### Before Moving
- [ ] **Passport**: Valid for duration of stay
- [ ] **Visa**: Apply 2-3 months in advance
- [ ] **Healthcare**: Private health insurance required initially; register with NVD after residency
- [ ] **HRT**: Get 6-month supply and doctor's letter (translated to Latvian)
- [ ] **Banking**: Consider Swedbank, SEB, or Citadele
- [ ] **Housing**: Research rental market (ss.lv, city24.lv)
- [ ] **Language**: Latvian essential for daily life; Russian widely spoken
- [ ] **Document translation**: Official Latvian translations of key documents needed

### After Arrival
- [ ] Register with PMLP (Office of Citizenship and Migration Affairs)
- [ ] Obtain personal code (Personas kods) — essential for all services
- [ ] Register for healthcare (NVD — National Health Service)
- [ ] Open local bank account
- [ ] Get local SIM card (LMT, Tele2, Bite)
- [ ] Connect with LGBTQ+ community (Mozaika, Baltic Pride)
- [ ] Find a GP and endocrinologist for HRT continuation

## Practical Information

### Cost of Living
- **Low to moderate** — one of the cheaper EU countries
- Monthly rent (1-bed): €300-600 (Riga), €200-400 (other cities)
- Monthly groceries: €250-350
- Public transport: €30/month (Riga)
- Dining out: €8-15 per meal

### Best Cities for LGBTQ+ Community
| City | LGBTQ+ Scene | Trans Healthcare |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Riga** | Moderate (few bars, venues) | Limited (RAKUS) |
| **Daugavpils** | Very limited | None |
| **Jurmala** | Very limited | None |

### Climate
- **Winters**: Cold (-5°C to -15°C), snow common, limited daylight (6-7 hours)
- **Summers**: Mild (15-25°C), long daylight
- **Spring/Autumn**: Transitional, rainy

### Language
- Official language: Latvian
- Russian: Very widely spoken (approx. 37% native speakers)
- English: Limited outside Riga and younger generations
- Free Latvian language courses available for immigrants
- Russian is very useful for daily life, especially outside Riga
