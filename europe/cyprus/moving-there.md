# Moving to Cyprus

## Immigration Pathways

### EU/EEA/Swiss Citizens

Cyprus grants full **free movement rights** to EU/EEA/Swiss citizens:

- **Up to 4 months**: No registration required
- **Over 4 months**: Must register with the Civil Registry and Migration Department for a **Yellow Slip** (registration certificate)
- **Requirements**: Valid passport or ID, proof of employment, sufficient funds, or health insurance
- **Right to work**: Automatic
- **After 5 years**: Eligible for permanent residence

### Non-EU Citizens

| Visa Type | Description | Requirements | Duration |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|----------|
| Work Visa | Employment with Cypriot employer | Job offer, employer sponsorship | 2 years, renewable |
| Student Visa | Full-time study | University acceptance, funds proof | Duration of studies |
| Family Reunification | Family of resident | Relationship proof, accommodation, income | 2 years, renewable |
| Permanent Residence (Investor) | Property purchase | €300,000+ property investment | Permanent |
| Temporary Residence | Self-funded individuals | Sufficient income, health insurance, accommodation | 2 years, renewable |

### Digital Nomad Visa

Cyprus launched a **Digital Nomad Visa** in 2022:

- Valid for up to **12 months**, renewable for another **2 years** (total 3 years)
- Must work remotely for a company outside Cyprus
- **Income requirement**: €3,500+/month (net)
- No tax liability in Cyprus for the first 5 years (if non-domiciled)
- Cannot work for Cypriot employers or clients
- Family members can accompany
- Application through the Civil Registry and Migration Department
- Quota of 500 visas initially (may be expanded)

## Moving Checklist

### Before Moving
- [ ] **Passport**: Valid for at least 6 months
- [ ] **Visa/Residence**: Check requirements for your nationality
- [ ] **Health Insurance**: EU citizens: EHIC; others: private insurance mandatory
- [ ] **HRT**: Get 3-6 month supply and doctor's letter (English or Greek)
- [ ] **Medical Records**: Translated into Greek if needed
- [ ] **Banking**: International banking options
- [ ] **Housing**: Research rental market (bazaraki.com, cyprusproperty.net)
- [ ] **Language**: English widely spoken; basic Greek helpful

### After Arrival
- [ ] **Register with Migration Department** for Yellow Slip (EU) or residence permit
- [ ] **Get ARC** (Alien Registration Certificate) — non-EU citizens
- [ ] **Register with GESY** (General Healthcare System)
- [ ] **Open bank account** — Bank of Cyprus, Hellenic Bank, Alpha Bank
- [ ] **Get Cypriot phone number** — Cyta, Epic, PrimeTel
- [ ] **Connect with Accept Cyprus** for LGBTQ+ community

### Language
- **Official languages**: Greek and Turkish
- **English**: Very widely spoken — Cyprus is one of the most English-friendly EU countries
- English is used in government, business, and daily life
- Many Cypriots speak English as a first or second language
- Greek is useful for integration but not essential for daily life

### Climate
- **Mediterranean climate**: Hot summers, mild winters
- **Summer**: 25°C to 40°C (June-September)
- **Winter**: 10°C to 20°C (December-February)
- **Sunshine**: Over 300 sunny days per year
- **Best time to arrive**: Spring or autumn

### Trans-Specific Considerations
- **Cyprus is socially conservative** — public displays of affection should be cautious
- **Nicosia and Limassol** are the most tolerant areas
- Rural areas and smaller towns are more traditional
- **Accept Cyprus** is the main LGBTQ+ organization providing support
- Limited trans healthcare — plan for accessing care abroad (Greece, UK)
- GESY system covers basic healthcare but not specialized trans care
- Legal gender recognition requires navigating the medicalized court system
- Growing expat community provides social support

### Important Note on Northern Cyprus
- The northern part of Cyprus is a **separate political entity** only recognized by Turkey
- Legal protections for trans people do not apply in the north
- Immigration to the north follows separate rules
- Most trans-friendly resources and protections exist in the Republic of Cyprus (south)
