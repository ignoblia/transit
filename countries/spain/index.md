## Legal Overview

Spain passed its landmark **Ley Trans (Law 3/2023)** in early 2023, making self-ID legal for all adults. The law was controversial within feminist and political circles but significantly improved the legal framework.

- **Gender Recognition:** Self-ID since Law 3/2023. Adults 16+ can self-declare at the Registro Civil. 14–16 with parental consent, 12–14 with judicial approval. No medical requirements, no surgery, no therapy, no court order. Process takes 3–6 months.
- **Anti-Discrimination Law:** Comprehensive. Gender identity is protected in employment, housing, and services. The 2023 law specifically strengthens protections.
- **Hate Crime Laws:** Gender identity included in the criminal code (Article 22.4, aggravated by discriminatory motives).
- **Same-Sex Marriage:** Legal since 2005 (third country in the world).
- **Conversion Therapy:** Banned (Law 3/2023 explicitly prohibits it).

### Key Laws
- Law 3/2023 (Ley Trans): Self-declaration, conversion therapy ban, anti-discrimination
- Law 13/2005 (Same-Sex Marriage)
- Criminal Code Article 22.4 (Hate crimes)


## Healthcare Reality

### Gender-Affirming Care
Spain's public healthcare system (SNS) covers gender-affirming care, but coverage varies significantly by autonomous community (region).

- **HRT Access:** Informed consent available in many regions, but some areas still gatekeep. Madrid, Catalonia, Basque Country, and Valencia have good access. Andalusia and rural areas are more variable.
- **HRT Cost:** Minimal on public system (€4–10 co-pay per prescription). €30–60/month private.
- **Endocrinologist Wait Times:** 2–6 months on public system in major cities. 1–3 weeks private.
- **Gender-Affirming Surgeries:** Covered by SNS but wait times are long (1–3 years). Private options at €6,000–20,000.
- **Regional Variation:** Catalonia and Basque Country have dedicated trans health units. Some regions (e.g., Extremadura, Castilla-La Mancha) have no specialised services, requiring travel.

### Trans-Friendly Clinics
- **Madrid:** Hospital Ramón y Cajal, Hospital Clínico San Carlos, Fundación Triángulo (support)
- **Barcelona:** Hospital Clínic (Unitat de Trastorns d'Identitat de Gènere), Hospital de Bellvitge, Cogam (LGBTQ+ centre)
- **Valencia:** Hospital La Fe, Lambda (LGBTQ+ organisation)
- **Bilbao:** Hospital de Basurto, Errespetuz (trans support group)
- **Seville:** Hospital Virgen del Rocío, limited trans services

### Important Notes
- Spain's **Digital Nomad Visa** (2023) is one of Europe's most accessible, but it is new and some consulates have inconsistent processing.
- The **Non-Lucrative Visa** allows residence without working. You must prove sufficient savings or passive income. Cannot convert to a work visa from within Spain.
- **Empadronamiento** (municipal registration) is needed for healthcare, school enrolment, and some other services.


## Community and Visibility

- **Trans community:** Large and well-organised. FELGTBI+ (statewide federation), Fundación Triángulo, Cogam (Madrid), Casal Lambda (Barcelona), Chrysallis (families of trans youth).
- **LGBTQ+ spaces:** Madrid's Chueca district is one of Europe's most famous queer neighbourhoods. Barcelona's Eixample (Gaixample) district. Valencia's Russafa area.
- **Social acceptance:** Very high in major cities. Pride in Madrid (Orgullo) is one of the largest in the world with over 1 million participants.
- **Dating pool:** Large. Apps like Tinder, Grindr, Lex, and Wapo are commonly used.


## What the Equaldex Score Misses

- **Regional variation** is massive. Catalonia and Madrid are excellent; rural areas can be conservative and isolated.
- **Healthcare access** depends entirely on which region you register in. Some regions have dedicated trans health units; others have nothing.
- **Economic reality:** Spain's youth unemployment is high. Local salaries (€1,200–2,000/month gross) are much lower than northern Europe.
- **Work culture:** Siesta hours vary by region. Bureaucracy can be slow and paper-based.
