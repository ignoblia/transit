# Healthcare & HRT in Belgium

## Access Model

Belgium uses an informed consent model for transgender healthcare. You can access hormone therapy through endocrinologists or specialised gender clinics without requiring a mental health assessment or referral. In practice, many doctors still request a letter from a therapist, but this is not a legal requirement.

Public healthcare (INAMI/RIZIV) covers a significant portion of transgender healthcare costs, including HRT, mental health support, and gender-affirming surgeries. Up to 85-95% of costs may be reimbursed for registered patients.

| Erasmus Hospital | Brussels | Gender clinic, HRT, surgical options |

## Prescription Portability

Belgium is an EU member state, so prescriptions from other EU/EEA countries are generally recognised. If moving from outside the EU, you will need to see a Belgian doctor to get a local prescription. Bring a doctor's letter (translated to Dutch, French, or German) documenting your current HRT regimen.

You can carry up to a 3-month supply of HRT medications for personal use when entering Belgium with a valid prescription.

## Surgical Options

Gender-affirming surgeries are available in Belgium and are largely covered by public health insurance. Major hospitals in Ghent, Leuven, and Brussels offer top surgery, bottom surgery, and facial feminisation. Wait times for surgery are typically 6-18 months depending on the procedure and hospital.
