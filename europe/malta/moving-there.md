# Moving to Malta

## Immigration Pathways

### EU/EEA/Swiss Citizens
As an EU member state, Malta grants free movement to EU/EEA/Swiss citizens. Registration with the authorities is required for stays over three months.

### Non-EU Citizens

| Visa Type | Description | Requirements | Duration |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|----------|
| Work Visa | Employment with Maltese employer | Job offer, Employment Licence from Jobsplus | 1 year, renewable |
| Nomad Residence Permit | Remote workers | €2,700/month minimum income, remote work proof | 1 year, renewable |
| Global Residence Programme | Wealthy individuals | Property purchase/rent, tax remittance | 5 years, renewable |
| Family Reunification | Family of residents | Relationship proof, sufficient income | 1 year, renewable |
| Student Visa | Full-time study | University acceptance, funds proof | 1 year, renewable |

## Key Relocation Steps

1. **Arrange accommodation** — Rental market is competitive; start searching early
2. **Register with Identity Malta** — For residence documentation
3. **Apply for residence card** — Non-EU citizens require this
4. **Register with health services** — Obtain an EU Health Insurance Card or register with the public system
5. **Open a bank account** — Required for employment and daily life
6. **Register with Jobsplus** — If seeking employment

## Cost of Living

Moderate to high, with significant regional variation:
- Rent (1-bedroom): €700-1,200/month
- Groceries: €300-500/month
- Public transport: Very cheap (€15-30/month pass)
- Health insurance: Public healthcare is free at point of use; private insurance €300-800/year

## Trans-Specific Relocation Tips

- **Malta is extremely safe** for transgender people — lowest transphobia rates in Europe
- **English is an official language** — all medical and legal processes available in English
- **Bring medical documentation** in English
- **Carry sufficient HRT medication** for at least 3 months
- **Contact MGRM** for community support upon arrival
- **Register with the Gender Wellbeing Clinic** early — proactive registration is best

## Language

- **Official languages**: Maltese and English
- **English is widely spoken** by nearly the entire population
- **Maltese** is useful for integration but not essential for daily life
- Citizenship requires a Maltese language test (typically after 5+ years of residence)
