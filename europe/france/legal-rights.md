# Legal Rights in France

## Gender Recognition

France adopted a self-declaration model for legal gender recognition in 2022, significantly simplifying the previous process. Adults can apply to the civil registry court (tribunal judiciaire) in their area. The application requires a simple declaration that their gender identity does not match their recorded sex. No medical diagnosis, surgery, or hormone therapy is required.

Processing time varies by court but is typically 1-3 months. The cost is minimal (court filing fee of approximately €20).

## Name Changes

First name changes can be done through the same civil registry process as gender recognition, or independently. You submit a request to the local civil registry office (mairie) along with justification. There is no fee for name changes.

## Anti-Discrimination Laws

France prohibits discrimination based on gender identity and gender expression in:

- **Employment**: All aspects of employment
- **Goods and services**: Housing, healthcare, public services
- **Education**: All levels
- **Media**: Hate speech and incitement are illegal

The Défenseur des Droits (Rights Defender) handles discrimination complaints.

## Hate Crime Laws

Gender identity is recognised as an aggravating factor for hate crimes. Transphobic crimes carry enhanced penalties. France has a national hate crime reporting system, and specialised police units handle LGBTQ+ hate crimes.
