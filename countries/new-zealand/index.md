## Legal Overview

- **Gender Recognition:** Self-ID since 2021 (Births, Deaths, Marriages, and Relationships Registration Act). Adults can change legal gender by statutory declaration. No medical requirements, no surgery, no therapy, no court order.
- **Anti-Discrimination Law:** Comprehensive under the Human Rights Act 1993, explicitly including gender identity.
- **Hate Crime Laws:** Gender identity is a protected category in sentencing.
- **Same-Sex Marriage:** Legal since 2013.
- **Conversion Therapy:** Banned (2022, one of the first countries to do so).

### Key Laws
- Births, Deaths, Marriages, and Relationships Registration Act (2021)
- Human Rights Act 1993 (amended to include gender identity)
- Conversion Therapy Prohibition Act (2022)


## Healthcare Reality

- **HRT Access:** Informed consent is widely available. Many GPs prescribe HRT directly. Dedicated clinics like the Gender Affirming Service (Auckland) handle complex cases.
- **HRT Cost:** NZD 5–15 per prescription (publicly subsidised). Private: NZD 30–60/month.
- **Wait Times:** 2–6 weeks for GP appointment. Specialist endocrinology: 1–3 months.
- **Surgery:** Public funding available but wait times are long (1–3 years). Private options exist (NZD 15,000–30,000).

### Key Resources
- **Gender Minorities Aotearoa:** The national trans organisation
- **OUTLine NZ:** LGBTQ+ support line


## Where to Live

- **Auckland:** Largest trans community. Most diverse and progressive. Very expensive (1-bedroom: NZD 1,800–2,600/month).
- **Wellington:** Strong trans community. Smaller, more affordable. 1-bedroom: NZD 1,500–2,200/month.
- **Christchurch:** Smaller community. More conservative feel. Cheaper. 1-bedroom: NZD 1,200–1,800/month.
- **Dunedin:** Very small community. Student city.

**Monthly budget (1 person):** NZD 3,000–5,000 (≈ €1,700–2,800).

## Known Risks

1. **Extreme remoteness** — expensive and time-consuming to travel anywhere
2. **Very high cost of living** — Auckland and Wellington comparable to London
3. **Housing crisis** in major cities
4. **Small job market** outside skilled professions
5. **Distance from support networks** if you are from Europe or the Americas
