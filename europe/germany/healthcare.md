# Healthcare & HRT in Germany

## Access Model

Germany does not have a single national model — access varies significantly by region and practitioner. Major cities like Berlin, Hamburg, and Cologne have many informed consent providers, while rural areas may require a therapist's referral letter (Indikationsschreiben). The S3 guideline for transgender healthcare supports informed consent, but implementation is uneven.

Public health insurance (Krankenkasse) covers HRT, therapy, and surgeries. A therapist's Indikationsschreiben is often required for insurance to cover HRT, though some endocrinologists prescribe on a self-pay informed consent basis.

## Prescription Portability

EU/EEA prescriptions are recognised in Germany. Non-EU arrivals need a German prescription. Use the Queermed directory to find trans-competent doctors. A medical history in German or English documenting your HRT is essential.

A 3-month supply of HRT for personal use is generally permitted with a valid prescription.

## Surgical Options

Gender-affirming surgeries are covered by public health insurance after approval (Kostenübernahme). Top surgery, bottom surgery, hysterectomy, and facial feminisation are available. Major surgical centres include Munich, Berlin, and Hamburg. Approval wait times are 3-6 months; surgical wait times are 6-18 months.
