# Moving to Hungary

## Immigration Pathways

### EEA/EU Citizens
- **Free movement rights** — no visa required
- **Up to 3 months**: No registration needed
- **Over 3 months**: Register with the Immigration Office (Országos Idegenrendészeti Főigazgatóság)
- **Right to work**: Automatic
- **Residence card**: Required for stays over 3 months

### UK Citizens (Post-Brexit)
- **Schengen Area**: Visa-free for up to 90 days in any 180-day period
- **Long-term residency**: Requires a residence permit:
  - Work permit (job offer from Hungarian employer)
  - Student visa (enrollment at Hungarian university)
  - Family reunification
  - White Card (remote work visa)

### Third-Country Nationals
| Visa Type | Requirements | Duration |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| Work Permit | Job offer from Hungarian employer | Up to 2 years, renewable |
| Student Visa | Enrollment at accredited institution | Duration of studies |
| Family Reunification | Family ties to Hungarian resident | Tied to sponsor's status |
| EU Blue Card | Highly skilled (salary ~€35,000+/year) | Up to 4 years |
| White Card | Remote work for foreign company | Up to 1 year, renewable |

## Digital Nomad / Remote Work
Hungary offers a **White Card** for digital nomads:
- For remote employees of non-Hungarian companies
- Valid for up to **1 year**, renewable once
- Requirements: Remote employment, income above threshold
- Also available for freelancers with foreign clients

## Moving Checklist

### Before Moving
- [ ] **Passport**: Valid for duration of stay
- [ ] **Visa**: Apply 2-3 months in advance (non-EU)
- [ ] **Healthcare**: Private health insurance initially; register with NEAK after residency
- [ ] **HRT**: Get 6-month supply and doctor's letter (translated to Hungarian)
- [ ] **Banking**: Consider OTP Bank, K&H, or Revolut
- [ ] **Housing**: Research rental market (ingatlan.com, alberlet.hu)
- [ ] **Language**: Hungarian essential for daily life and bureaucracy
- [ ] **Document translation**: Official Hungarian translations needed

### After Arrival
- [ ] Register with Immigration Office (non-EU) or municipality (EU)
- [ ] Obtain Hungarian address card (lakcímkártya)
- [ ] Obtain TAJ number (health insurance card)
- [ ] Register with NEAK (National Health Insurance Fund)
- [ ] Open local bank account
- [ ] Get local SIM card (Telekom, Vodafone, Yettel)
- [ ] Connect with LGBTQ+ community (Háttér Society, Transvanilla)

## Practical Information

### Cost of Living
- **Moderate** — mid-range EU costs
- Monthly rent (1-bed): €400-800 (Budapest), €300-500 (Debrecen), €250-400 (Pécs)
- Monthly groceries: €250-350
- Public transport: €30/month (Budapest)
- Dining out: €8-15 per meal

### Best Cities
| City | LGBTQ+ Scene | Trans Healthcare |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Budapest** | Moderate but under pressure | Only real option |
| **Debrecen** | Very limited | None |
| **Szeged** | Very limited | None |

### Climate
- **Winters**: Cold (-5°C to 5°C), snow common
- **Summers**: Warm to hot (25-35°C)
- **Best time to arrive**: Spring or early autumn

### Language
- Official language: Hungarian (Uralic, unrelated to neighboring languages)
- English: Moderate in Budapest, limited elsewhere
- German: Somewhat understood in western areas
- Hungarian is very challenging to learn and essential for bureaucracy
