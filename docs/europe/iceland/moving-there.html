Moving to Iceland ​

Immigration Pathways ​

EEA/EU Citizens ​

  • Free movement rights — no visa required
  • Up to 3 months: No registration needed
  • Over 3 months: Must register with Registers Iceland
  • Right to work: Automatic, no work permit needed

UK Citizens (Post-Brexit) ​

  • Visa-free: Up to 6 months for tourism/short stays
  • Longer stays: Apply for residence permit:
    • Work permit (requires job offer)
    • Study permit (accepted to accredited institution)
    • Family reunification

Third-Country Nationals ​

  • Visas Required: Yes — apply through Directorate of Immigration
  • Options include:
    Visa TypeRequirementsDuration
    Work PermitJob offer from Icelandic employerUp to 2 years, renewable
    Student VisaEnrollment at accredited institutionDuration of studies
    Remote Work VisaRemote employment, min income requirementsUp to 18 months
    Family ReunificationFamily ties to Icelandic residentPermanent
    Start-up VisaInnovative business plan, funding securedUp to 2 years

Digital Nomad / Remote Work ​

Iceland offers a long-term visa for remote workers:

  • Valid for up to 18 months
  • Requirements: Remote employment with income above threshold (~1,000,000 ISK/month)
  • Cannot be extended beyond 18 months
  • Good option for testing living in Iceland before committing

Moving Checklist ​

Before Moving ​

  • [ ] Passport: Valid for at least 6 months
  • [ ] Visa: Check requirements for your nationality
  • [ ] Healthcare: Research health insurance options
  • [ ] HRT: Get 3-6 month supply and doctor's letter
  • [ ] Banking: Set up international banking options
  • [ ] Housing: Research rental market (reykjavikrent.is)
  • [ ] Language: Basic Icelandic helpful; English widely spoken

After Arrival ​

  • [ ] Register with Registers Iceland (EEA citizens: after 3 months)
  • [ ] Apply for kennitala (Icelandic ID number)
  • [ ] Register with healthcare system
  • [ ] Open local bank account
  • [ ] Get Icelandic phone number
  • [ ] Connect with LGBTQ+ community (Trans Iceland, Samtökin '78)

Practical Information ​

Cost of Living ​

  • High — especially housing in Reykjavík
  • Monthly rent (1-bed apartment): 150,000-250,000 ISK
  • Monthly groceries: 50,000-80,000 ISK
  • Public transport: 6,000-10,000 ISK/month

Climate ​

  • Winters: Cold (0°C to -10°C), dark (4-5 hours daylight)
  • Summers: Mild (10°C to 15°C), midnight sun
  • Best time to arrive: Late spring/summer (May-August)

Language ​

  • Official language: Icelandic
  • English: Widely spoken, especially in Reykjavík
  • Icelandic courses available for immigrants
