# Moving to Italy

## Immigration Pathways

### EEA/EU Citizens
- **Free movement rights** — no visa required
- **Up to 3 months**: No formalities
- **Over 3 months**: Register with the Anagrafe (municipal registry) and obtain a Codice Fiscale (tax code)
- **Right to work**: Automatic, no work permit needed
- **Residenza**: Official residence status after registration

### UK Citizens (Post-Brexit)
- **Schengen Area**: Visa-free for up to 90 days in any 180-day period
- **Longer stays**: Apply for residence permit:
  - **Work visa**: Job offer from Italian employer
  - **Elective Residence Visa**: Proof of sufficient passive income (€31,000+/year)
  - **Student Visa**: Enrollment at Italian university
  - **Family Reunification**: Family ties to Italian resident
  - **Golden Visa**: Investment of €500,000+ (bonds) or €250,000+ (start-up)

### Third-Country Nationals
| Visa Type | Requirements | Duration |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| Work Visa (Subordinato) | Job offer from Italian employer | 1-2 years, renewable |
| Elective Residence Visa | Sufficient passive income (~€31,000/year) | 1 year, renewable |
| Student Visa | Enrollment at accredited institution | Duration of studies |
| Family Reunification | Family ties to Italian resident | Tied to sponsor's status |
| Golden Visa | Investment (€250k-€500k+) | 2 years, renewable |
| EU Blue Card | Highly skilled (salary ~€30,000+/year) | Up to 4 years |

## Digital Nomad / Remote Work
Italy does **not have a dedicated digital nomad visa**. Remote workers typically use:
- **Elective Residence Visa**: For those with passive income (pensioners, investors, remote workers with high savings)
  - Minimum income: ~€31,000/year (varies by consulate)
  - Does NOT allow work for Italian companies
  - Valid for 1 year, renewable
  - Can transition to permanent residency after 5 years
- **Work Visa**: If employed by an Italian company
- **Freelance Visa (Lavoro Autonomo)**: For self-employed professionals, requires a business plan and client base

## Moving Checklist

### Before Moving
- [ ] **Passport**: Valid for duration of stay
- [ ] **Visa**: Apply 2-4 months in advance at Italian consulate
- [ ] **Healthcare**: Private health insurance required for visa; register with SSN after residency
- [ ] **HRT**: Get 3-6 month supply and doctor's letter (translated to Italian)
- [ ] **Banking**: Consider Intesa Sanpaolo, UniCredit, or Fineco
- [ ] **Housing**: Research rental market (immobiliare.it, idealista.it)
- [ ] **Language**: Italian essential for bureaucracy and daily life
- [ ] **Document translation**: Apostille and official translations of key documents

### After Arrival
- [ ] Apply for Permesso di Soggiorno (residence permit) — non-EU
- [ ] Register with Anagrafe (municipal registry)
- [ ] Obtain Codice Fiscale (tax code) — essential for everything
- [ ] Register with SSN (Servizio Sanitario Nazionale) for healthcare
- [ ] Open local bank account
- [ ] Get local SIM card (TIM, Vodafone, WindTre, Iliad)
- [ ] Find a GP (Medico di Base) for referrals
- [ ] Connect with LGBTQ+ organizations (MIT, Arcigay)
- [ ] Locate the nearest gender clinic (Careggi Florence, San Camillo Rome)

## Practical Information

### Cost of Living
- **Moderate** — varies significantly by region
- Monthly rent (1-bed):
  - Milan: €800-1,400
  - Rome: €600-1,000
  - Florence/Bologna: €600-900
  - Naples/South: €400-700
- Monthly groceries: €250-400
- Public transport: €35-50/month

### Climate
- **North**: Cold winters (0-10°C), warm summers (20-30°C)
- **Central**: Mild winters (5-15°C), hot summers (25-35°C)
- **South/Islands**: Mild winters (10-20°C), very hot summers (30-40°C)

### Language
- Official language: Italian
- English: Limited outside tourist areas and tech industry
- Italian essential for bureaucracy, healthcare, and daily life
- Free Italian language courses available through CPIA (Centri Provinciali Istruzione Adulti)
