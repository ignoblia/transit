# Moving to Luxembourg

## Immigration Pathways

### EU/EEA/Swiss Citizens
Luxembourg, as an EU member state, grants free movement rights to citizens of EU/EEA countries and Switzerland. EU citizens can enter Luxembourg with a valid ID card or passport. After three months of residence, registration with the commune is required.

### Non-EU Citizens

| Visa Type | Description | Requirements | Duration |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|----------|
| Work Visa (Salarié) | For employment with a Luxembourg-based company | Job offer, employment contract, qualification recognition | 1 year, renewable |
| EU Blue Card | For highly qualified workers | Degree + salary threshold (approx. €58,000/year) | Up to 4 years |
| Family Reunification | For family members of Luxembourg residents | Relationship proof, sufficient income, accommodation | 1 year, renewable |
| Job Seeker Visa | For job searching in Luxembourg | Proof of qualifications, sufficient funds, accommodation | 3 months |
| Student Visa | For full-time study | University acceptance, sufficient funds | 1 year, renewable |

## Key Relocation Steps

1. **Secure accommodation** — Housing is very expensive and scarce; start searching early
2. **Register with the commune** — Within 8 days of arrival (for stays over 3 months)
3. **Apply for a residence permit** — Non-EU citizens must apply before arrival
4. **Register for health insurance** — CNS registration is automatic for employees
5. **Open a bank account** — Required for salary and many administrative processes
6. **Learn one of the official languages** — French is most practical for daily life; Luxembourgish for citizenship

## Cost of Living

Luxembourg has one of the highest costs of living in Europe:
- Rent (1-bedroom in city centre): €1,500-2,200/month
- Groceries: €400-600/month for one person
- Public transport: Free throughout the country
- Health insurance: Covered by CNS (employee contributions ~3.05% of salary)

## Trans-Specific Relocation Tips

- **Bring copies of medical records** translated into French or German
- **Carry at least 3 months of HRT medication** initially, with a doctor's letter
- **Register with a GP** near your residence; they can refer to endocrinology at CHL
- **Connect with CIGALE** for community support and trans-specific guidance
- **Luxembourg City is very safe** but smaller towns may have less awareness

## Language

- **Official languages**: Luxembourgish, French, German
- **French** is most commonly used in administrative and medical contexts
- **English** is widely spoken in business and at hospitals
- **Luxembourgish** is required for citizenship (oral exam)
- Language classes are subsidised by the state
