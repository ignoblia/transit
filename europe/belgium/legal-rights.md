# Legal Rights in Belgium

## Gender Recognition

Belgium adopted a self-declaration model for legal gender recognition in 2018, replacing the previous requirement for surgery and sterilisation. A trans person can change their legal gender on official documents by submitting a declaration at the civil registry office. Minors aged 16-17 can apply with parental consent; those under 16 require a court order.

No medical diagnosis, surgery, or hormone therapy is required. The process involves a simple administrative fee and typically takes a few weeks.

## Name Changes

Legal name changes follow a simplified procedure for transgender people. You submit a request to the civil registry office along with your gender recognition declaration. The process costs approximately €50-100 and takes 1-3 months.

## Anti-Discrimination Laws

Belgium has strong anti-discrimination protections. Gender identity and gender expression are explicitly protected grounds in:

- **Employment**: Hiring, promotion, termination
- **Goods and services**: Housing, healthcare, banking
- **Education**: All levels of schooling
- **Social protection**: Benefits and social security

The Belgian Institute for Equality of Women and Men handles discrimination complaints.

## Hate Crime Laws

Gender identity is explicitly recognised as an aggravated factor in hate crime prosecutions. Penalties for crimes motivated by transphobia can be doubled. Belgium has a dedicated hate crime reporting system through local police and the Institute for Equality.
