# Moving to Romania

## Immigration Pathways

### EEA/EU Citizens
- **Free movement rights** — no visa required
- **Up to 3 months**: No registration needed
- **Over 3 months**: Register with the General Inspectorate for Immigration (IGI)
- **Right to work**: Automatic
- **Residence card**: Issued for stays over 3 months

### UK Citizens (Post-Brexit)
- **Schengen Area**: Visa-free for up to 90 days in any 180-day period
- **Long-term residency**: Requires a residence permit:
  - Work permit (job offer from Romanian employer)
  - Student visa (enrollment at Romanian university)
  - Family reunification
  - Digital Nomad Visa

### Third-Country Nationals
| Visa Type | Requirements | Duration |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| Work Permit | Job offer from Romanian employer | Up to 1 year, renewable |
| Student Visa | Enrollment at accredited institution | Duration of studies |
| Family Reunification | Family ties to Romanian resident | Tied to sponsor's status |
| EU Blue Card | Highly skilled (salary ~€35,000+/year) | Up to 2 years |
| Digital Nomad Visa | Remote work, income threshold | Up to 1 year, renewable |

## Digital Nomad / Remote Work
Romania offers a **dedicated digital nomad visa** (since 2021):
- Valid for up to **1 year**, renewable
- Requirements:
  - Remote work for a company outside Romania
  - Monthly income of at least €3,300 (3x gross average salary)
  - Valid health insurance
  - Clean criminal record
- Can bring family members
- Cannot work for Romanian employers
- Good option for testing living in Romania before committing

## Moving Checklist

### Before Moving
- [ ] **Passport**: Valid for duration of stay
- [ ] **Visa**: Apply 2-3 months in advance (non-EU)
- [ ] **Healthcare**: Private health insurance initially; register with CNAS after residency
- [ ] **HRT**: Get 6-month supply and doctor's letter (translated to Romanian)
- [ ] **Banking**: Consider BCR, BRD, ING, or Revolut
- [ ] **Housing**: Research rental market (imobiliare.ro, olx.ro)
- [ ] **Language**: Basic Romanian helpful; moderate English in cities
- [ ] **Document translation**: Official Romanian translations needed

### After Arrival
- [ ] Register with IGI (non-EU) or municipality (EU)
- [ ] Obtain Romanian CNP (personal numeric code) — essential for all services
- [ ] Register with CNAS (National Health Insurance House)
- [ ] Open local bank account
- [ ] Get local SIM card (Orange, Vodafone, Digi)
- [ ] Connect with LGBTQ+ community (ACCEPT)
- [ ] Register with Institutul de Endocrinologie for trans healthcare

## Practical Information

### Cost of Living
- **Low** — one of the most affordable EU countries
- Monthly rent (1-bed): €300-600 (Bucharest), €250-450 (Cluj-Napoca), €200-400 (Timișoara), €200-350 (Iași)
- Monthly groceries: €200-300
- Public transport: €15-25/month (Bucharest)
- Dining out: €6-12 per meal

### Best Cities for LGBTQ+ Community
| City | LGBTQ+ Scene | Trans Healthcare |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Bucharest** | Active, growing scene | Only comprehensive option |
| **Cluj-Napoca** | Moderate scene | Limited |
| **Timișoara** | Small scene | None |
| **Iași** | Very limited | None |

### Climate
- **Winters**: Cold (-5°C to 5°C), snow common
- **Summers**: Warm to hot (25-35°C)
- **Spring/Autumn**: Mild and pleasant
- **Best time to arrive**: Spring or early autumn

### Language
- Official language: Romanian (Romance language, closest to Italian)
- English: Moderate in Bucharest and Cluj, limited elsewhere
- French: Somewhat understood by older generations
- Romanian is a Romance language and relatively accessible for English/French/Italian speakers
