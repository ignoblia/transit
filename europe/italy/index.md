# Italy

## Quick Verdict
Italy has a long history of trans legal recognition (Law 164/1982) but the process remains medicalized and requires court authorization. Healthcare access varies significantly between progressive northern cities and conservative southern regions.

**Overall Rating: ⭐⭐½ (2.5/5)**

### Why Italy?
- **Legal**: Medicalized — requires court authorization, diagnosis, and HRT/surgery (Law 164/1982)
- **Healthcare**: Gatekeeping at Careggi (Florence) and San Camillo (Rome); limited options
- **Safety**: Moderate — Vatican influence and conservative government; urban areas more tolerant
- **Community**: Active trans community in Rome, Milan, Bologna, Naples
