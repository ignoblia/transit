## Legal Overview

- **Gender Recognition:** Self-ID since 2018 (Supreme Court ruling). No surgery or therapy required. Change at registry office.
- **Anti-Discrimination Law:** Supreme Court rulings provide protections. Federal anti-discrimination bill pending.
- **Hate Crime Laws:** 2019 Supreme Court ruling criminalises homophobia and transphobia (pending federal legislation).
- **Same-Sex Marriage:** Legal since 2013 (Supreme Court ruling).


## Healthcare

- **HRT Access:** Informed consent available. Public SUS system covers gender-affirming care by law.
- **Cost:** Free on SUS. Private: BRL 100–400/month (≈ €18–72).

## Cost of Living (São Paulo/Rio)

1-bedroom: BRL 2,000–4,500/month (≈ €360–800). Monthly budget: BRL 5,000–10,000 (≈ €900–1,800). Very affordable for those earning foreign currency.


## Key Considerations

- **⚠️ Situation rapidly changing:** Strong legal framework but rising political violence
- **Highest trans murder rate in the world** — Brazil is extremely dangerous for trans women, especially those in sex work
- **Inflation and economic instability** affect daily life
- **Portuguese essential** for integration
- **Large, visible trans community** in São Paulo and Rio
- **Asylum:** Not recommended; safety concerns outweigh legal protections for most
