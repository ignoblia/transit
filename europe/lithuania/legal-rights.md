# Legal Rights in Lithuania

## Gender Recognition
Lithuania has a restrictive gender recognition system made more difficult by a 2020 Constitutional Court ruling:

- **Requirements**: Diagnosis of gender dysphoria + HRT + sex reassignment surgery
- **Constitutional Court Ruling (2020)**: The court ruled that a person who has changed gender must be considered as having the "acquired sex," but this also reinforced the medicalized model
- **Age**: 18+ only
- **Process**: Application through the Civil Registry (Civilinės metrikacijos skyrius) with medical certification
- **Surgery**: Effectively required for legal recognition

### The Process
1. Diagnosis from a psychiatrist or gender clinic (Santaros Clinics, Vilnius)
2. Minimum 1-2 years of documented HRT and real-life experience
3. Sex reassignment surgery
4. Court or administrative application for gender marker change
5. Updated identity documents

**Note**: The Constitutional Court ruling has been interpreted conservatively by lower courts, making legal gender change harder to obtain. Several cases have been rejected or delayed.

## Name Changes
- Name change is processed through the **Civil Registry**
- Can be done separately from gender marker change in some cases
- Requires a valid reason — gender transition is generally accepted
- Processed within 1-3 months

## Anti-Discrimination Laws
- **Employment**: Prohibited under the Labour Code
- **Goods and Services**: Protected under the Equal Treatment Law
- **Education**: General non-discrimination provisions apply

The **Law on Equal Treatment** prohibits discrimination based on sexual orientation but does not explicitly list gender identity. The protection is often interpreted as covering gender identity by extension.

## Hate Crime Laws
- The **Criminal Code** includes hate crime provisions covering race, religion, nationality
- Sexual orientation is included as a protected characteristic
- Gender identity is **not explicitly mentioned**
- A 2019 amendment to add gender identity failed to pass
- Some municipalities have local anti-discrimination measures

## Document Recognition
- Updated passports and ID cards are recognized internationally
- Previous documents are archived but not publicly accessible
- Lithuanian passports are valid for travel within the Schengen Area
- Some trans people report difficulties with document checks at borders
